## Project intent

A workhorse zippered tote sized for groceries, gym kit, or a day's
worth of tools — built once, replaced never. 12-oz waxed canvas
exterior gives the bag water resistance and the patina that gets
better with age; cotton-twill lining keeps the inside soft; bound
seams hide raw edges and reinforce the high-stress corners.

**Finished dimensions:** 18" wide × 14" tall × 6" deep (volume ≈
1,512 in³ / ~25 L).

**Construction features:**
- Top-zip closure (#10 YKK molded coil, two-way pull).
- Two padded shoulder straps, 1.5" wide × 24" drop, foam-cored.
- Inside slip pocket, 8" × 10", on the back-wall lining.
- Bound seams throughout (1" double-fold cotton bias tape).
- Boxed corners (6" gusset, mitered base).
- Bottom reinforcement panel: doubled canvas + optional 1/8" HDPE
  stiffener (sourced separately — see `sourcing.csv`).

## Headline question — industrial walking-foot machine

**Yes, you'll want it.** 12-oz waxed canvas (single layer) is fine
on a domestic machine with a 90/14 jeans needle, but the high-stress
seams in this build go through 4 layers of canvas + binding, plus the
strap anchors that go through 6+ layers. A domestic machine with a
standard presser foot will slip, skip stitches, and likely break
needles at the corners.

The textile area at Maker Nexus has both domestic and industrial
machines (see `tools.md` `#textile-area`). The industrial walking-foot
machine is what this build is designed around. It uses a compound feed
(needle + presser foot + feed dog all move together) so thick stacks
feed evenly without shifting.

**Cert path:** Maker Nexus's current cert structure (per
`spaces/maker-nexus/certifications.md`) groups all sewing/textile
equipment under a single `#textile-cert`, granted by the
`#class-textile-intro` (2 hours). The intro class explicitly covers
"domestic and industrial sewing machines, sergers, basic maintenance"
(see `classes.md`).

**You said you have your domestic-machine cert** — that almost
certainly means you already hold the umbrella `#textile-cert` as
currently scoped. **Verify before you assume:** Maker Nexus is
gradually splitting some shop areas into sub-certs (cf. metalshop's
lathe/mill/welder split). If textile gets a "industrial-machines"
sub-cert, you may need a short check-out with a textile-area steward
before independent walking-foot use. Ask the front desk or post in
the textile-area channel.

**If a sub-cert exists and you don't hold it:** the workaround is
to do all single- and double-layer seams on the domestic machine,
then book a buddy session with a textile steward for the four
high-stack ops (strap anchors, gusset corners, binding cross-overs,
zipper end-tabs). That keeps you moving while you queue for the
sub-cert class.

## Build time estimate

- Pattern + cutting: ~2 hours
- Construction: ~6 hours (4 hours if you're fast, 8 if you're
  binding for the first time)
- Total: ~8 hours, single session feasible if you batch your
  pressing.
