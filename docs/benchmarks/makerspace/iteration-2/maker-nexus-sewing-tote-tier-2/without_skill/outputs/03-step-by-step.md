# Step-by-Step Assembly

Estimated total time: **6-9 hours** spread across 2-3 sessions. The walking-foot machine is in high demand on weekday evenings; book a Saturday morning block if possible.

Legend:
- **[D]** = can do on your domestic machine
- **[W]** = use the walking-foot industrial
- **[H]** = handwork

## Session 1 — Prep and sub-assemblies (~2 hr)

### Step 1. Cut everything [D-prep]
Per `02-cut-list.md`. Label each panel on the wrong side with chalk: F (front), B (back), L/R gusset, lining versions. Trust the labels later when everything looks identical.

### Step 2. Make the slip pocket [D]
1. Fold the 9 x 15 lining piece in half right sides together — you now have a 9 x 7.5 rectangle.
2. Sew around three sides with 1/2 in SA, leaving a 3 in turning gap on the bottom.
3. Trim corners, turn right side out, press flat.
4. Topstitch the top edge 1/4 in from fold.
5. Pin (yes, pins are fine on lining) to the lining back panel, centered horizontally, top edge ~5 in from the top of the panel.
6. Edgestitch around three sides (sides + bottom only — top stays open). Backstitch heavily at the top corners — this is where pocket failure happens.

### Step 3. Build the straps [W]
This is your **first walking-foot task** and a great warmup.
1. Center foam (3 x 12) on the wrong side of the canvas wrap (4 x 25), leaving 6.5 in of canvas on each end with no foam.
2. Lay webbing on top of foam, centered.
3. Fold the long edges of the canvas wrap in 1/2 in, then fold the whole thing in thirds lengthwise so the webbing+foam is enclosed and the long edges meet at center back. Clip every 2 in.
4. **Walking-foot, Tex 40 thread, stitch length 3.5 mm, 110/18 needle.** Topstitch 1/8 in from BOTH long edges down the full strap length. Backstitch at start and end.
5. Optional: a third row of stitching down the center for a tailored look.
6. Repeat for second strap.

The unwrapped 6.5 in of canvas at each strap end is your attachment tab — this gets sandwiched into the bag's top seam later.

## Session 2 — Bag body (~3 hr)

### Step 4. Attach straps to exterior front and back [W]
1. On the exterior front panel (right side up), measure 5 in in from each side edge and mark vertical strap placement lines.
2. Position the strap so the unwrapped 6.5 in tab extends from the top edge of the panel down 4 in. The padded length of the strap arches above the panel.
3. Clip in place. Confirm: both straps should arch the same direction.
4. Walking-foot topstitch the tab to the panel: an X-box pattern (rectangle + diagonals) in a 1.5 x 4 in box. This is the strap anchor — it bears all the load.
5. Repeat for the back panel.

### Step 5. Install the zipper [W]
The zipper sits in a faced opening between the two top edges. This is the hardest single step in the build.
1. Take one zipper-facing strip (19 x 2.5). Press 1/2 in to the wrong side along one long edge.
2. Place this folded edge along one side of the zipper tape, right sides up. Clip.
3. Switch to the **zipper foot on the walking-foot machine** (Maker Nexus has one — ask the steward). Stitch 1/8 in from the fold.
4. Repeat with the second facing strip on the other side of the zipper tape. You now have a zipper sandwiched between two facing strips.
5. With right sides together, sew each long raw edge of this zipper-and-facing assembly to the top edge of the front panel, and the other side to the top edge of the back panel. SA 1/2 in.
6. Press the seams toward the body panels. Topstitch 1/4 in from the seam on the body side, catching the SA underneath.

### Step 6. Assemble the exterior bag body [W]
1. With the front+zipper+back assembly opened up flat, attach the bottom (if you cut three panels) or skip if you cut a single wrap.
2. Attach the gussets: right sides together, clip a gusset to one side of the front panel from top to bottom; sew at 1/2 in. Pivot at the bottom corner. Continue along the bottom. Pivot. Continue up the other side to the back panel top.
3. Repeat for second gusset.
4. **Open the zipper halfway right now** — if you forget, you cannot turn the bag right side out later. (Yes, you've been told. You'll still forget. That's why we say it twice.)
5. Bind every interior seam allowance with 1 in bias tape (see Step 8).

### Step 7. Assemble the lining [D]
Identical to the exterior assembly but:
- Skip the bias binding — serge or zigzag the SAs instead.
- Leave a 6 in gap in one bottom seam for turning.
- Domestic machine is fine; lining is only 2 layers of twill.

## Session 3 — Joining and finishing (~2 hr)

### Step 8. Bind the exterior seams [W or H]
Bias binding on a curve-free, straight-seam bag can be done by machine on the walking-foot:
1. Open one bound seam allowance flat against the inside of the bag.
2. Lay open bias tape along the SA, raw edges aligned with the SA raw edge. Stitch in the first fold crease (1/4 in from raw edge).
3. Wrap the bias around the SA edge to the other side. Clip.
4. Stitch in the ditch from the public side, catching the bias fold underneath.

If machine binding feels like too much, hand-finish the second pass with a slip stitch. Slower but bulletproof.

### Step 9. Bag the lining [W]
1. Turn the exterior right side out (through the open zipper — you DID open it, right?).
2. Slip the lining (still wrong-side-out) over the exterior so right sides face. Align top edges and side seams.
3. Sew around the top seam where lining meets zipper facing — you're sewing through lining + zipper-facing seam allowance. SA 1/2 in.
4. Pull the entire bag through the gap you left in the lining bottom in Step 7.
5. Slip-stitch the lining gap closed (or machine-stitch — it won't show).
6. Push lining into bag. Press the top edge with a pressing cloth and LOW heat (waxed canvas + heat = redistributed wax. A hair dryer on cool to soften creases is safer than an iron.)

### Step 10. Final topstitch [W]
Topstitch around the entire top edge of the bag, 1/4 in from the seam. This locks the lining down and gives the bag its finished crispness.

### Step 11. Inspection checklist [H]
- [ ] Both straps anchored with X-box stitches; tug-test each one
- [ ] Zipper opens and closes smoothly across full travel
- [ ] All exterior SAs bound; no raw waxed-canvas edges visible inside
- [ ] Slip pocket securely attached at bottom corners
- [ ] No chalk marks remaining on either side
- [ ] Bag stands up on its own when empty (gusset stiffness check)

You're done.
