# Troubleshooting

## Symptom: Skipped stitches on canvas
**Most likely cause:** dull or wrong-type needle, or fabric not feeding evenly.
**Fix:**
1. Change the needle. Even a "new" needle that has sewn 100 inches of canvas is dull. Swap it for a fresh 110/18.
2. Check that you're on a jeans or topstitching needle, not a universal.
3. Confirm the walking foot is engaged (lever down). Easy to forget.

## Symptom: Thread keeps breaking
**Most likely cause:** tension too tight, or needle eye too small for the thread.
**Fix:**
1. Loosen top tension by 0.5.
2. Verify needle is 110/18 minimum for Tex 40 thread.
3. Re-thread from scratch — including lifting the presser foot during threading so the tension discs open.

## Symptom: Wax is rubbing off in patches and the canvas looks shiny
**Most likely cause:** too much pressure from the presser foot, or you ironed the right side directly.
**Fix:**
1. Ease foot pressure.
2. For ironing damage, use a hair dryer on warm to redistribute the wax. Rub gently with a soft cloth.
3. If a panel is badly affected, you can re-wax it with Otter Wax Fabric Wax bar after the bag is finished.

## Symptom: The boxed corner looks puckered/crooked
**Most likely cause:** gusset and body panel weren't aligned when you started the seam, or you didn't pivot precisely at the corner.
**Fix:**
1. Mark the pivot point with chalk on both pieces BEFORE sewing. They should kiss exactly at the corner.
2. When pivoting: needle down, presser foot up, rotate 90°, presser foot down, continue. No "almost-90" — exactly 90.
3. If it's already sewn and bad, seam-rip from 1 in before the corner to 1 in after, and re-sew that 2 in section only.

## Symptom: Zipper feels gritty or sticks
**Most likely cause:** wax has migrated onto the zipper teeth, or the slider is catching on the facing fold.
**Fix:**
1. Run the slider back and forth several times — this distributes any wax.
2. If still gritty, rub the teeth with a candle stub or beeswax bar (yes, more wax — but the right kind).
3. Inspect the facing edge near the slider; if the fold is catching, restitch that ~1 in section closer to the teeth.

## Symptom: Bag won't stand up on its own
**Most likely cause:** insufficient gusset structure, or seams not topstitched.
**Fix:**
1. Topstitch gusset seams from the outside, 1/4 in from each side of the seam. This adds rigidity.
2. Optional: add a removable bottom stiffener — cut a piece of plastic canvas or 1/8 in HDPE to 17 x 5.5 in, slip into the bottom of the lining cavity. (Easiest done if you build a slip pocket into the lining bottom for it.)

## Symptom: I sewed the lining gap shut before turning the bag
**Most likely cause:** missed Step 7 instruction.
**Fix:** Open a 6 in section of one lining bottom seam with a seam ripper. Turn. Slip-stitch closed. No one will ever see.

## Symptom: I sewed the bag inside-out
**Most likely cause:** zipper installation step got flipped.
**Fix:** Seam-rip the zipper out, flip the panels, re-install. Adds ~45 min. Welcome to sewing.

## Symptom: Strap pulled out of the X-box stitch under load
**Most likely cause:** stitch density too low, or canvas-only strap tab (no webbing reinforcement reaching into the X-box area).
**Fix:**
1. Confirm the webbing core extends fully through the X-box, not just the canvas wrap.
2. Re-stitch the X-box twice — once at 3.5 mm stitch length, once at 2.5 mm directly over it.
3. For a true repair, add a 2 x 4 in canvas reinforcement patch over the X-box on the inside, sewn through all layers.

## When to stop and ask a steward
- Thread is bird-nesting under the throat plate (do NOT tug — lift presser foot, lift needle, gently extract from above)
- Needle hit metal (zipper, hardware) and may be bent — stop immediately, change needle
- Machine is making a new sound it didn't make 10 minutes ago
- You've ripped the same seam more than twice — fresh eyes help
