# Finishing and Care

## First-day finishing
1. **Trim every thread tail.** Use sharp scissors right against the fabric. Tex 40 thread is visible.
2. **Hand-rub the seams flat.** The wood-clapper or seam-roller works the wax back into the seam channels.
3. **Hit any stubborn creases with a hair dryer on warm,** rubbing gently with a cloth as the wax softens.
4. **Photograph it.** You made a bag. The wax is at its richest and most even right now.

## Breaking in
Waxed canvas softens dramatically over the first 2-4 weeks of use:
- Carry it loaded (~5-10 lb) every day
- Don't baby it — the marks and creases are the point
- The wax will redistribute toward the high-friction areas (corners, strap-attachment points) and the fabric will get more pliable

You'll know it's broken in when the bag holds the shape of whatever you put in it without springing back to crisp-rectangle.

## Long-term care

### Cleaning
- **Spot-clean only.** Damp cloth with cold water. Add a tiny amount of mild soap (Dr. Bronner's or saddle soap) for stubborn marks.
- **Do not machine-wash.** It will strip the wax and felt the canvas.
- **Do not dry-clean.** Solvents dissolve the wax.

### Re-waxing (every 1-2 years for daily-use bags)
1. Buy an Otter Wax Fabric Wax bar (or equivalent) — about $14
2. Warm the bag in indirect sun for 20 minutes so the existing wax softens
3. Rub the bar across the canvas in firm, even strokes — apply to the whole bag, not just worn spots
4. Use a hair dryer on warm to melt the new wax in
5. Wipe off any excess with a clean cloth
6. Let cure for 24 hours before use

### Stitching repair
- Inspect the strap X-boxes every 6 months under a strong light. If a stitch is broken, restitch immediately — adjacent stitches go fast once one fails.
- The bound seams are the highest-durability part of the bag and rarely need attention.
- Zipper failure: usually the slider, not the tape. Replacement sliders for #5 and #8 zippers are cheap; YouTube has 100 tutorials on the swap.

### What to expect over time
- **Year 1:** wax dulls, color softens, creases set in at the gussets and corners
- **Year 2-3:** bag becomes noticeably more flexible; wax may need refreshing at high-wear spots
- **Year 5+:** with care, this bag outlives most consumer bags 3x over. Re-wax annually after year 3.
