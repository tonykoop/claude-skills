## What the metrics don't show

The Insights report is a snapshot of one 23-day window of one project. Things it doesn't capture:

- **The cross-vendor coordination.** The 1,393 Claude messages are only one side; Codex personas were running in parallel during many of those sessions.
- **The pre-existing skill-authoring time.** The skills published in this repo represent ~6 months of incremental development before the measurement window opened.
- **The non-coding work.** Writing handoff prompts, debugging hooks, designing the dispatch contract, and recovering from codex pane failures don't show up directly in the lines-of-code counts but are most of what makes the system work.

The metrics are a useful credibility anchor — *this volume of shipped work is real* — but they're a lower bound on the system's effort, not an upper bound.
