# Safety Notes

For a parent/grandparent + child build at a makerspace. Order: laser-cut
hazards (adult only), assembly hazards (kid-OK with supervision), field
hazards.

## Laser cutter operation — adult only

- **Never leave the machine unattended while cutting.** Phenolic-bonded
  Baltic birch is more flammable than MDF; flare-ups are sudden.
- **Active fume extraction to outside.** Phenolic-glue smoke contains
  formaldehyde and phenol vapor.
- **Air assist on. CO₂ fire extinguisher within arm's reach.**
- **Eye protection** appropriate to the laser wavelength.
- **No reflective material on the bed.** Aluminum, mirror-finish steel,
  mirrored stickers — any of these can reflect the beam back into the
  optics.
- **Vacuum the bed of resin dust before each run.**

## Glue and finish — adult-led, kids may participate

- Titebond III is FDA-rated food-safe once cured but not fun to ingest
  wet. Wash hands after gluing.
- **Linseed oil rags spontaneously combust.** Flatten oil-soaked rags on
  a non-flammable surface to dry; do not ball them up in a trash can.
- **Do not finish the inside.** Welfare gate G2.

## Assembly — kid-friendly with supervision

- **Edge sanding before assembly:** every laser-cut edge has a thin dark
  char layer. Hand kids 220-grit and let them gently knock the char off
  visible edges. Best 10 minutes of the build for a young child. Wear a
  dust mask, work outside or under extraction.
- **Pilot drilling with a hand bradawl** is preferred over a power drill
  for kids — slower, controllable, no spinning chuck near fingers.
- **Pivot-screw step:** an adult drives those four screws (over-driving
  cracks the ply). Kids hand-fit the door and verify it swings.

## Field installation — adult only, kids observe

- **Lag screws into a post** with a powered impact driver: kickback can
  twist a wrist. Brace against the post.
- **Ladders ≥ 6 ft:** standard ladder safety. Have a spotter. Carry the
  box up tied to a belt loop, not in a hand.
- **Pole-setting:** if concrete is used, it cures over hours; do not
  load the post with a box until next day.
- **Wasp removal** from a previous box happens at dawn on a cool morning,
  not as a kid-supervision activity.

## Animal-welfare safety (welfare gates G1–G7)

These are not safety risks to humans, but they are reasons the box can
fail the bird:

- **G1 — Never** add a perch.
- **G2 — Never** finish the inside.
- **G6 — Never** install on a pole without a predator baffle.
- **Never** use pressure-treated lumber for the mount post within 4 ft
  of the box.
- **Always** confirm clear flight path from entrance to the nearest woody
  cover.
- **Always** clean annually. A second-year nest on top of last year's
  nest brings parasite loads that kill broods.
