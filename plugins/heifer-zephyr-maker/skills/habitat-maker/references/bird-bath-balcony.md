# Bird-Bath And Balcony Habitat Reference

Use this reference when a `habitat-maker` prompt asks for a bird bath, balcony
water source, renter-safe habitat, or hot-climate patio deployment. The output
should be a maintenance-first welfare packet, not a decorative object.

## Default Balcony Pattern

Default to a low, ballasted, removable basin for compact balconies:

- Basin: shallow saucer or dish, about 12-14 in outside diameter.
- Placement: morning sun or bright shade, protected from afternoon heat where
  possible.
- Water depth: 1/8-1/4 in at the entry edge, 5/8-3/4 in maximum, and never a
  slick open area deeper than 1.0 in without nearby footing.
- Footing: textured walking surface plus an emergent ramp, island, or broad
  rough stone that clearly reaches the rim.
- Stability: 12-16 lb of ballast below the basin, outside the bird-contact
  water path, with rubber feet or another non-skid base.
- Service: removable basin that can be dumped and scrubbed in under 5 minutes.

Reject railing-clamp baths, tall pedestals, pump-first designs, and deep
decorative bowls unless the prompt gives a specific reason and the packet
documents the added stability, splash, wiring, cleaning, and evaporation burden.

## Bird-Bath Welfare Gates

Every bird-bath packet must include these pass/fail gates.

| Gate | Pass condition | Fail remedy |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Shallow depth | Entry edge is 1/8-1/4 in deep and maximum intended fill is 5/8-3/4 in. | Lower fill line, add a ramp/island, or replace the basin. |
| Textured footing | Birds can stand on a textured surface from edge to deepest zone. | Add bird-safe texture, a rough stone, or a removable textured insert. |
| Escape path | A ramp, island, or rough stone creates an obvious walk-out path to the rim. | Add a tied-to-rim escape feature; do not rely on loose pebbles alone. |
| Dump/scrub cadence | Packet requires daily dump/refill in hot weather and scrub at least 2-3 times weekly. | Reduce scope or choose a design easier to remove and clean. |
| Mosquito prevention | No standing water is left through missed-maintenance periods; travel mode is dry. | Dump before travel or remove the bath until service resumes. |
| Water-contact material safety | All water-contact surfaces are known bird-safe and cleanable. | Replace unknown, treated, corroded, or flaking surfaces. |
| Heat/evaporation | Packet names sun exposure, refill cadence, and heat-wave behavior. | Move to shade/morning sun, reduce volume exposed to heat, or suspend use. |
| Stability | Basin cannot skate, tip, or blow over under expected balcony wind and bump tests. | Lower the center of mass, add ballast below the basin, or reject the site. |

## Balcony And Renter Checklist

Balcony packets must make the deployment constraints explicit.

- **No-drill anchoring.** Use ballast, captured seats, non-skid feet, trays, or
  freestanding bases. Do not require screws into railings, walls, or shared
  building surfaces.
- **Wind/tip resistance.** Keep the bath low and broad; avoid tall pedestals,
  narrow bases, and railing-mounted bowls for v1.
- **Drip control.** Place the bath in a tray or no-drip zone. Confirm overflow
  cannot reach downstairs neighbors, facade surfaces, or walkways.
- **Window-strike posture.** If near glass, place it very close to the window
  or use visible strike mitigation. Do not create a fast flight corridor into
  reflective glass.
- **Railing/neighbor constraints.** Keep the bath set back from the balcony
  edge and avoid over-rail hardware, loose objects, or splash paths.
- **Travel-dry behavior.** Dump, scrub, and leave dry before travel, heat-wave
  absences, or any period when daily service is uncertain.

## Material Safety Matrix

| Material | Status | Use guidance |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Lead-free ceramic/glaze | Preferred if known food-safe or outdoor-safe | Use smooth cleanable glaze for water contact; add removable rough footing. Reject chipped, crazed, or unknown vintage glaze. |
| Concrete | Conditional | Use only cured, sealed-with-bird-safe-product, non-crumbling concrete. Rinse well and monitor pH/alkalinity concerns before use. |
| Natural stone | Preferred/conditional | Good for ramps and islands if stable, cleanable, and not sharp. Avoid crumbly or oily stone. |
| BPA-free hard plastic | Conditional renter option | Acceptable when sturdy, UV-stable enough for the season, easy to scrub, and not brittle or flaking. |
| Stainless steel | Conditional | Cleanable and corrosion-resistant, but often slick and heat-prone; add textured footing and shade. |
| Copper alloys | Reject for bird-contact water | Avoid copper, brass, bronze, and unknown copper alloys in standing bird water. |
| Galvanized metal | Reject for bird-contact water | Avoid zinc-coated surfaces in standing bird water, especially when worn, acidic, or corroded. |
| Treated wood | Reject for water contact | Do not use pressure-treated or chemically treated wood where birds stand or water sits. |
| Paint/sealer | Conditional/reject | Only use products explicitly safe for water-contact wildlife use after full cure; otherwise keep out of water-contact surfaces. |
| Unknown glaze | Reject until identified | Treat thrifted, vintage, handmade, cracked, or unlabeled glaze as unknown unless provenance confirms safety. |

## Compact Deployment Checklist

Use this as the minimum field checklist for a bird-bath packet.

- [ ] Basin is shallow: 1/8-1/4 in entry edge and 5/8-3/4 in maximum fill.
- [ ] No slick open area deeper than 1.0 in without immediate footing.
- [ ] Textured footing reaches the deepest zone.
- [ ] Ramp, island, or broad stone gives a walk-out path to the rim.
- [ ] Water-contact materials pass the material safety matrix.
- [ ] Basin lifts out for dumping and scrubbing in under 5 minutes.
- [ ] Daily hot-weather dump/refill plan is documented.
- [ ] Travel mode is dry.
- [ ] Ballast sits below the basin and outside bird-contact water.
- [ ] Gentle bump test passes without skating, sloshing over, or tipping.
- [ ] No drill, clamp, or over-rail dependency is required for renter use.
- [ ] No drip path reaches neighbors, walkways, or building surfaces.
- [ ] Window-strike posture is addressed if glass is nearby.
- [ ] Food is kept away from the bath unless the packet accepts extra traffic
      and cleaning.

## Optional Fill-Depth Gauge Template

Include this text or a simple printable strip when a prompt asks for a gauge.

```text
BALCONY BIRD-BATH FILL GAUGE

0 in      basin floor
1/8 in    entry edge target
1/4 in    entry edge maximum
5/8 in    preferred deepest zone
3/4 in    maximum fill line
1 in      reject unless textured footing is immediately adjacent

Mark the 3/4 in line outside the water-contact surface. Do not paint, ink, or
seal a mark where birds stand or drink unless the product is confirmed safe for
wildlife water contact after full cure.
```

## Agent Record Requirements

When producing a bird-bath packet, record:

- Balcony context: sun exposure, wind exposure, railing/edge constraints, drip
  path, glass proximity, and renter/no-drill constraints.
- Material decision: selected basin, footing, ballast, and any rejected
  materials.
- Maintenance decision: dump/refill cadence, scrub cadence, mosquito
  prevention, and travel-dry plan.
- Validation run: pass/fail checklist, bump test, fill-line check, and first
  deployment observation notes.
