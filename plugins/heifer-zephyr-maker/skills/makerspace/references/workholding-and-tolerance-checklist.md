# Workholding And Tolerance Checklist

Use this checklist before shipping any jig, fixture, or machine setup plan.

## Datums

- What is the primary locating surface?
- What prevents rotation?
- What defines the secondary and tertiary references?
- Does the datum scheme match how the feature is inspected later?

## Clamping and restraint

- What prevents lift?
- What prevents side slip?
- What prevents chatter or vibration?
- Is clamp force applied where the part can actually take it?
- Will clamps block cutter, nozzle, beam, or probe access?

## Tool access

- Is there enough cutter reach without unsafe stick-out?
- Can chips, dust, coolant, or smoke escape?
- Is there sacrificial backing where breakout or burrs matter?
- For lasers, how are small parts retained after final contouring?

## Tolerance checkpoints

- What is checked before first cut?
- What is checked on the first article?
- What is checked during the batch?
- What is the stop-work threshold if drift appears?

## Documentation minimum

Every workholding plan should state:

- datums
- clamping method
- anti-rotation feature
- tooling
- inspection method
- failure watchlist
