# Auld Lang Syne — intermediate (denser ornaments)

Same melody as `01-easy/auld-lang-syne/`. Same range, same form,
same tempo. The principal-note contour is identical so a player
can graduate from one to the other phrase by phrase.

## What changed

| Element | 01-easy | 02-intermediate |
|---|---|---|
| Doublings | bars 1, 3, 9, 11 only | **every** quarter-length principal note |
| Birls | absent | `{GAGA}A2` on the repeated low A's |
| Grips | absent | `{GBG}B` between repeated B's |
| High-A density | one doubling per climax | doublings on every high-A and adjacent G/F# |
| Cadence | `{GBG}A8` | grip-into-cadence + final `{GBG}A8` |

## What stayed the same

- Principal melody — every audible note in the easy setting is in
  this setting at the same beat.
- Tempo (♩ = 80), key (A mixolydian), form (16-bar through).
- Range — still all nine chanter notes, no transposition.

## Practice tips

1. **Birls take separate practice.** `{GAGA}A2` is *low-A → low-G →
   low-A → low-G → low-A* squeezed into a single beat. Practice it
   in isolation at half tempo before adding it to the tune.
2. **Two-octave parallel.** Bars 6 and 14 both climb F# → high-A in
   one beat with a doubling on the high-A. Get those two bars solid
   first, then walk back to the start.
3. **Memorial vs. New Year revisited.** This setting is more
   grandiose than the easy one. Save it for performances; for
   gatherings the easy version is friendlier to sing along to.
4. **No rests, ever.** Note bars 4 and 12 (`{GeG}e6 {ge}e2`): two
   high-E doublings under a single eight-eighth held tone, not a
   rest in sight.

## Range check

All nine chanter notes: G, A, B, c (=C#), d, e, f (=F#), g, a.
G appears throughout the grace clusters; B appears as a principal
in the descents and inside grip clusters.
