# Loch Lomond — intermediate (denser ornaments)

Same melody as `01-easy/loch-lomond/`. Same range, same form, same
tempo. The principal-note contour is identical so a player can
graduate from one to the other phrase by phrase.

## What changed

| Element | 01-easy | 02-intermediate |
|---|---|---|
| Doublings | every long note in phrase opening | **every** quarter-length principal note |
| Throw on D | absent | every D-after-lower-pitch |
| Grips | absent | between repeated principals (e.g., `{GAG}A`) |
| Cadence | `{GBG}A8` | `{GBG}A6 {ge}e2 → {GBG}A8` (grip-into-cadence) |

## Practice tips

1. **Sight-read the easy version first.** This setting is the same
   tune phrase for phrase. If the easy version is not yet steady,
   come back to this one.
2. **One ornament family at a time.** Add the throws first (every D
   that follows A or B). Then the grips. Then the doublings on the
   shorter notes. Don't try to layer all of it at once.
3. **The grip is a balance test.** The `{GAG}A` grip is two G grace
   notes around an A — it sounds like a *flick*. Don't slow the
   tune down for it; the grip lives inside the principal's beat.
4. **Cadence — the grip-into-cadence is the showstopper.** Bar 8
   ends with `{GBG}A6 {ge}e2`: low-A held under a grip, then a
   high-E pickup into the second half. The high-E is a *brightener*,
   a momentary lift before the long rest of the second phrase.

## Range check

All nine chanter notes appear: G, A, B, c (=C#), d, e, f (=F#), g, a.
G and B appear primarily inside grace clusters.
