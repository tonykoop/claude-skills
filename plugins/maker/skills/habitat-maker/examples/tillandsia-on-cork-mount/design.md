# Design — Tillandsia on cork-bark mount (canonical minimal packet)

A wall-hung air-plant sculpture: one or more *Tillandsia* nestled into virgin
cork bark, mounted so the plants stay removable for weekly soaking. This is the
canonical minimal example for the [`tillandsia-on-bark`](../../references/tillandsia-on-bark.md)
reference. Non-cavity, hand-mounted — prose + sketch are the artifact of record;
no generator script (no laser/CNC geometry).

## Intent

- Display 1–3 small/medium Tillandsia (e.g. *T. ionantha*, *T. juncea*) on a
  vertical cork-bark slab, bright-indirect-light spot.
- Plants must lift out (or the slab lifts off the wall) for ~weekly soak.
- Renter-friendly: single picture hook, no heavy fixture.

## Substrate

- Virgin cork bark slab, roughly 200 × 120 × 25 mm (natural, varies).
- No sealant on the front/contact face. Optional matte sealant on the **back**
  only, to slow moisture wicking into the wall hanger.

## Mounting method

- **Crevice nest** for any natural hollow that holds a plant unaided (preferred,
  zero hardware).
- **Tie-down** for plants without a nesting spot: coated/nylon-jacketed wire or
  monofilament fishing line around the **lower stem only**, threaded through a
  small drilled hole and twisted at the back. Reversible for soaking.
- No adhesive in this example (keeps every plant removable).

## Orientation

- Hung vertically with a slight forward lean at the base so water runs off the
  front face; no cupped pocket that traps water against a plant base.

## Hanging

- One stainless sawtooth hanger or a single keyhole slot on the back, sized for
  the wet mass of cork + soaked plants (see BOM). Light enough for a standard
  picture hook; no wall fixture needed. If scaled up, route the hanger to
  `makerspace`.

## Light note (display, not care)

- Bright indirect light. Detailed watering/soak schedule and specimen tracking
  route to the `houseplant` skill — this packet only states the placement need.
