## Will the impression scar permanently?

Short answer: almost certainly fades, will not scar permanently.

What you're describing — a *slight impression on the underside, no bite-in* — is the best-case outcome. The wire pressed into the bark but didn't break the cambium or get overgrown. On *Ficus benjamina*, which has thin, smooth, fast-renewing bark and is in active warm-season growth right now (27°C, full morning sun is prime growing weather), here's what typically happens:

- **Next 2–6 weeks:** the indentation softens visibly as the branch resumes radial growth and the bark plumps back out.
- **2–4 months:** mark fades to a faint helical shadow, often only visible at certain light angles.
- **By next growing season:** usually indistinguishable from surrounding bark on a vigorous ficus.

The thing that *does* scar permanently is bite-in — when bark grows *over* the wire and you have to cut it out, leaving a spiral groove or ridge. You avoided that. A pressure mark with intact bark is cosmetic and temporary.

One caveat: if the underside mark is on what will become the *front* of the tree, photograph it now at a consistent angle so you can confirm fading rather than guessing later.
