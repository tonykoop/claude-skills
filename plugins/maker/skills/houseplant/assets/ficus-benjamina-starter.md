# Ficus benjamina 01

A multi-year ficus being trained slowly into a bonsai. Notable trunk character: thick, braided/intertwined exposed aerial roots at the base. Currently approaching a structural prune.

## Current State

- Trunk: braided aerial-root nebari, robust foundation.
- Canopy: top-heavy; apex starting to dominate.
- Interior: likely shaded out; back-budding inside the canopy should be encouraged after structural pruning.

## Care Preferences

- **Water:** check soil moisture by finger or chopstick test. Allow the top inch to dry between waterings; never let the root ball desiccate.
- **Fertilizer:** balanced or slightly high-N during active growth (spring through early autumn). Pause in winter dormancy. Reduce or pause for 7 days after structural pruning.
- **Light:** bright indirect indoors; will tolerate direct morning sun outdoors. Avoid sudden moves from low light to direct hot afternoon sun (leaf burn).
- **Temperature:** keep above ~10°C / 50°F. Move indoors before first cold snap.
- **Humidity:** appreciates higher humidity, especially for aerial root development. Misting and humidity tents acceptable when actively guiding aerial roots.

## Ficus-Specific Notes

- **Wire windows are short.** Ficus cambium thickens fast in warm-season growth. Plan to inspect wire every 1–2 weeks during active growth and remove or reapply before bark swells over wire.
- **Back-budding is strong.** Hard structural cuts are typically tolerated on healthy specimens during active growth; the plant will push new buds along older wood.
- **Latex sap.** Cuts will weep white latex. Seal cleanly with a sterile cut paste if available; keep latex off skin and surfaces.
- **Aerial roots are a feature.** Treat them as design elements, not nuisance growth. Guide promising tips with damp sphagnum, straws, or tubes when the plant is healthy and warm.
- **Flowers are hidden.** Ficus flowers internally inside the syconium ("fig"). Do not expect visible blooms; the bud/bloom tracker should not be set up for visible flowering on this species.
