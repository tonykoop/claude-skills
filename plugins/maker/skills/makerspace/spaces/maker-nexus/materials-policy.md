# Materials policy — Maker Nexus

These rules apply to the Maker Nexus shop. Verify against the current
wiki / staff before any build that depends on a borderline call.

## Universal bans (anywhere in the shop, on any tool)

- Chlorinated plastics (PVC, vinyl) — produces hydrochloric acid;
  damages optics on the laser, lungs everywhere.
- ABS on the laser — cyanide-bearing fumes.
- Polycarbonate on the laser — toxic.
- Anything fluoride-bearing on the laser (PTFE / Teflon).

## Tool-specific bans

- **CNC router (`#cnc-area`):** banned: aluminum, steel, brass, any
  ferrous. The shop's CNC is wood-shop-grade. Metal CNC work belongs
  in the metalshop area on a mill or metal-rated machine.
- **Laser (`#laser-area`):** banned list above. Verify any borderline
  material with staff before cutting.
- **Tablesaw / jointer / planer:** wood and engineered woods only.
  No metal, no plastic, no laminate-in-the-cut path.

## Bring-your-own materials

Most stock at Maker Nexus is member-supplied. Common sourcing:
- Hardwood: MacBeath in Berkeley
- Plywood: MacBeath, Crosscut Hardwoods, or local big-box
- Sheet plastic: TAP Plastics (multiple Bay Area locations)
- Steel / aluminum: Online Metals, Industrial Metal Supply

The shop typically stocks only consumables (some glue, sandpaper,
shop fasteners). Check the consumables shelf on arrival.

## Disposal policy

- Sawdust: shop dust collection, emptied by staff.
- Plastic offcuts: take home or use the marked recycling bin.
- Metal offcuts: scrap bin in the metalshop.
- Hazardous waste (resin, solvents, finishes): take to community
  hazardous waste — Sunnyvale's Smart Station or county equivalent.
- Used blades / bits: the broken-tooling bin near each machine.

## Updates

When materials policy changes, update both this file and the
relevant `banned_materials` lists in `profile.yaml`.
