# Safety rules — Maker Nexus

This is a starter snapshot of generic shop-safety expectations. The
authoritative source is the Maker Nexus orientation and any posted
signage in each shop area. Verify on-site.

## PPE — always

- Safety glasses any time a tool is running.
- Closed-toe shoes anywhere on the shop floor.
- Hearing protection in machine areas (the shop provides foam
  earplugs; bring your own muffs if you prefer).

## PPE — task-dependent

- **Dust mask / respirator** — sanding, MDF cutting, finishing,
  resin handling.
- **Cut-resistant gloves** — not on rotating spindles (lathe, drill
  press, mill); fine for hand work.
- **Welding hood + leather apron + leather gloves** — any welding
  process.
- **Resin gloves (nitrile, not latex)** — SLA / resin handling.

## Dust collection

- Tablesaw, jointer, planer: connect to central dust collection
  before turning on. Don't run these on shop vac alone.
- CNC router: dust shoe must be installed for any wood operation.
- Sanders: dust mask + central collection or dedicated shop vac.

## Emergency

- E-stop locations: every machine has one; familiarize yourself
  during orientation.
- Fire extinguishers: posted at intervals around each shop area.
- First aid kit: front desk and in each shop area.
- Emergency exits: marked with signage; verify on first visit.

## Buddy system

Some operations require a second person on the floor:
- Welding (any process) — eye-protection for bystanders matters.
- Lathe over 12" diameter.
- Operations where the user is new and the cert holder asked for
  observation.

## Solo / after-hours work

24/7 access for members in good standing. Solo after-hours work
follows the same buddy rules as during open hours. Check the
on-call channel before solo work that involves higher-risk
operations.

## Reporting incidents

Any injury, near-miss, or equipment damage gets logged in the
incident system. Reporting is not punitive — it's how the shop
learns.
