# Measurement Request Checklist

Ask for the fewest new measurements that would change the analysis. Prefer measurements that retire multiple unknowns at once.

## Universal Photo Requests

- Add a ruler, caliper jaws, coin, grid mat, known fastener, or other scale reference in the same plane as the feature.
- Capture front, back, left, right, top, and bottom views with the camera centered and far enough away to reduce perspective distortion.
- Add close-ups of seams, joints, fasteners, labels, wear marks, hinges, springs, pivots, ports, cracks, repairs, and material transitions.
- Include one photo showing the whole object and one photo showing each critical interface.
- For shiny, transparent, dark, or fuzzy surfaces, add side lighting and one image with glare intentionally shifted.

## Dimension Requests

- Overall length, width, height, thickness, weight, and center-of-mass estimate if handling matters.
- Diameter, wall thickness, hole spacing, slot width, thread size, fastener head size, and repeated-part pitch.
- Clearance around moving parts and the range of motion at each joint.
- Contact surface dimensions, gasket/seal dimensions, bearing surfaces, and acoustic openings.
- Tolerance-sensitive features: fits, gaps, alignment pins, registration edges, datum faces.

## Mechanisms

- Short video of the mechanism moving slowly through its full travel.
- Still photos at start, mid-travel, end, and any detent/lock position.
- Force direction: push, pull, twist, lift, squeeze, gravity-loaded, spring return, magnetic catch.
- Evidence of hidden parts: screw covers, snap tabs, seams, access panels, lubrication, wear tracks, witness marks.

## Instruments and Acoustic Artifacts

- Bore, cavity, membrane, string, bridge, reed, soundhole, or resonator dimensions.
- Material thickness at vibrating or resonant surfaces.
- Tuning references: recorded pitch, spectrum, string gauges, membrane tension, reed gap, air path.
- Photos of mouthpiece, bridge, nut, saddle, soundboard, bracing, bore exits, and internal cavities when accessible.
- Environmental notes: temperature, humidity, and whether measurements were taken assembled or disassembled.

## Soft Goods and Wearables

- Flat measurements with the item relaxed and under light tension.
- Seam allowances, stitch spacing, zipper length, hardware spacing, strap width, lining thickness.
- Inside-out photos if available.
- Material hand: stretch direction, nap direction, stiffness, weight, backing, interlining, padding.

## Electronics or Enclosures

- External ports, labels, switches, vents, screws, clips, seals, and board access points.
- Voltage/current labels, battery markings, connector pin counts, wire colors, and component IDs.
- Non-destructive inspection first. Ask before destructive teardown.

## Builder Handoff Minimums

Before handing off to a builder skill, request any missing item that is builder-critical:

- One reliable scale reference or direct overall dimensions.
- Critical interface dimensions and tolerances.
- Material or acceptable substitutes.
- Load, motion, acoustic, electrical, thermal, or environmental requirements.
- Safety constraints and intended use.
