## Decisions Made

| Decision | Choice | Rationale | Confidence |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Alloy | 5052-H32 | Skill ref flags 6061-T6 as crack-prone in bends and 7075-T6 worse. 5052 cold-bends without cracking and is forgiving for a kid-facing build. | medium |
| Thickness | 0.063 in | Matches the skill's beetleweight planning thickness (0.062 in 6061 baseline mapped to 5052 inventory). | medium |
| Bend radius | R = T = 0.063 in | Skill default for sheet metal; conservative; verify on coupon. | medium |
| K-factor | 0.45 | Mid-range planning value for common aluminum per skill ref. | medium |
| Architecture | 4 parts (tub + wedge + top + 2x guards) | Single removable top panel enables under-60-second service. Separable guards are consumable spares. | medium |
| Fasteners | M3 socket-head cap with nylock | Kid-friendly tool standard; nylocks tolerate vibration without thread locker. | medium |
| No welding | Yes | Family-build constraint; team can re-build between matches. | high |
| Wedge angle | 18 to 22 deg (default 20) | Inside the skill's 30 to 45 deg "sloped front armor" range when measured from the floor — wedge angle (top deck slope) and armor angle are related; wedge face leading edge slopes upward at 20 deg from floor toward rear. | low |
