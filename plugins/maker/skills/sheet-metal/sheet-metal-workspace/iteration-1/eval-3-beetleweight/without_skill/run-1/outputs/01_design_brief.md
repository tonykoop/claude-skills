# Beetleweight Wedge Chassis — Design Brief

**Bot Name (placeholder):** Niece Force One
**Weight Class:** Beetleweight (3 lb / 1361 g)
**Drivers:** Two middle-school nieces (novice / first-season builders)
**Design Philosophy:** Forgiving, repairable, "drives well, takes a hit, fixes in 5 minutes."

## Design Goals (in priority order)
1. **Survivability** — Wedge geometry deflects hits; forgiving aluminum that bends instead of shattering.
2. **Service access** — Top plate off in under 60 seconds, batteries swappable between matches.
3. **Driver confidence** — Low, stable wedge shape that self-rights against most opponents.
4. **Wheel protection** — No exposed tire sidewalls; guarded against horizontal spinners.
5. **Build forgiveness** — Folded-sheet construction, no welding, kid-friendly hardware (M3 button head).

## Why This Approach for Middle Schoolers
- **Folded aluminum** instead of CNC pocketed plate: bends absorb energy, dents are repairable with a vise and a soft mallet.
- **5052-H32 aluminum**, not 7075: 7075 is stronger but cracks on impact. 5052 yields, deforms, and keeps going. This is the right call for a novice team that will take hits.
- **Wedge primary weapon**: no spinning weapon means no precision balancing, no belt tensioning, no weapon ESC tuning. Drive practice becomes the differentiator — which is exactly what new drivers need.
- **All fasteners are M3 button-head socket cap**: one hex key, one spares baggie.
