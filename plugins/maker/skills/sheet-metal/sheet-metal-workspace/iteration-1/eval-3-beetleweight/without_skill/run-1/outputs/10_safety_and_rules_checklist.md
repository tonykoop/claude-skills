# Safety, Tech-Inspection, and Tournament Rules Checklist

Print this. Put it on the pit table. Check items off before every match.

## Pre-Tournament Safety (one-time)
- [ ] Bot weighs under class limit (1361 g for beetleweight 3 lb) on official scale
- [ ] All fasteners installed with thread locker
- [ ] Arming link physically removes power when out (verified with multimeter)
- [ ] LED indicator shows power status visibly through top deck
- [ ] No exposed sharp edges except legal wedge front (within rule radius)
- [ ] Battery is intact, no swelling, no exposed wires, fits firmly in pocket
- [ ] LiPo bag present for transport and charging
- [ ] Spare arming link in kit
- [ ] Failsafe configured on receiver: motors stop on signal loss (CRITICAL)
- [ ] Driver's transmitter bound to ONE bot only during tournament

## Pre-Match Checklist (every match)
- [ ] Bot is fully assembled, all bolts checked
- [ ] Battery fully charged and properly seated
- [ ] Arming link is OUT
- [ ] Drive test on pit floor passed
- [ ] Wheels spin freely, no rub on guards
- [ ] Wheel guards inspected for cracks or deformation
- [ ] Wedge front edge inspected — straighten if dented
- [ ] Transmitter battery checked, antenna up
- [ ] Spare battery charged and in pit kit
- [ ] Both drivers know which one is driving this match

## Arena Procedure
1. Walk bot to arena holding it by the chassis (NOT by a wheel).
2. Place bot on the start square.
3. Insert arming link AFTER placing.
4. Step back behind the safety line.
5. Wait for the safety official's GO signal.
6. After the match, wait for the "all stop" call.
7. Pull arming link BEFORE picking up bot.
8. Walk bot back to pit; do not handle a hot bot.

## Failure Modes to Watch For
| Symptom | Likely Cause | Action |
|---|---|---|
| Bot only drives one direction | Failsafe stuck or stick trim issue | Re-bind RX, check trim, reset failsafe |
| One wheel doesn't turn | Motor wire popped or set screw loose | Open pit, reflow solder, retighten |
| Bot loses signal mid-match | RX antenna pinched against aluminum | Re-route antenna outside Faraday cage |
| Wedge front rides off floor | Front rake too high, or skid pad missing | Reseat UHMW pad, check ground gap |
| Battery puffy after match | Over-discharge, low-voltage cutoff failed | DO NOT charge. Dispose properly. New battery. |
| Smoke from inside the shell | ESC overheating or wire short | Pull arming link IMMEDIATELY. Open shell, inspect, do not re-arm until cause found. |

## Things That Will Get You Disqualified
- Driving outside the arena with the arming link in
- Picking up an armed bot
- Charging a damaged LiPo
- Modifying a bot mid-bracket without re-tech-inspection
- Driving a bot that fails the failsafe test

## Niece-Specific Coaching Notes
- It is OKAY to lose a match. The bracket is double-elimination at most events.
- Drive conservatively the first match — you are learning the arena floor, the lights, and the latency.
- Aim the wedge at the opponent's WHEELS, not their weapon. Wedge under, lift, push to the wall.
- If your wedge is on top, you are winning. Stay on top.
- High-five each other after every match, win or lose. This is what makes a team.
