# Assembly & Installation Checklist

## Phase 1 — Procurement & verification (Day 0)
- [ ] Confirm 2024 RAV4 Prime trim has raised side rails (XSE/SE do; LE may not — verify)
- [ ] Run Yakima Fit My Car at yakima.com with VIN — confirms LandingPad 22 and SkyLine combo
- [ ] Order all items in BOM (lead time ~7 days for metal, 2–3 days for Yakima)
- [ ] Measure your actual roof rail length and spacing; compare to assumed 58 in / 38 in
- [ ] Weigh your ShiftPod bag on a bathroom scale (model varies)

## Phase 2 — Foundation install (Day 1, 30 min)
- [ ] Wash roof and rails; dry thoroughly
- [ ] Assemble LandingPad 22 onto SkyLine towers per Yakima instructions
- [ ] Place crossbar 1 at **8 in from front of roof** (in front of sunroof)
- [ ] Place crossbar 2 at **46 in from front of roof** (behind sunroof)
- [ ] Hand-tighten tower clamps, then torque to **9 N·m (80 in·lb)** per Yakima spec
- [ ] Install SKS lock cores if purchased
- [ ] Drive 5 miles; re-torque clamps

## Phase 3 — Frame fabrication (Day 2, 2–3 hr at makerspace)
- [ ] Cut F-01 (2x at 52 in), F-02 (2x at 40 in), F-03 (3x at 40 in) on miter saw with non-ferrous blade
- [ ] Deburr all cuts with file or deburring wheel
- [ ] Lay out frame on flat table: 2x F-01 long sides, 2x F-02 ends, 3x F-03 internal at 13/26/39 in from one end
- [ ] Square the frame with framing square (diagonals equal)
- [ ] Install corner brackets at each F-01/F-02 junction (4 corners, 2 brackets each = 8)
- [ ] Install tee brackets at each F-03 to F-01 junction (6 joints, 1 bracket each)
- [ ] Tighten all M8 hardware to **15 N·m**, anti-seize on threads

## Phase 4 — Deck skin (Day 2, 1 hr)
- [ ] If not pre-perforated: nest holes in CAD, plasma/water-jet cut 3/8" perf in 1/2" staggered pattern
- [ ] Trim skin to 52 x 42 in
- [ ] Deburr all edges (CRITICAL — sharp Al will cut hands and strap webbing)
- [ ] Lay skin on frame; clamp
- [ ] Mark rivet pattern: every 4 in around perimeter, every 6 in along internal crossmembers
- [ ] Drill 13/64 (3/16) holes through skin and into top of extrusion
- [ ] Set pop rivets

## Phase 5 — Fairing (Day 2, 1 hr)
- [ ] Cut S-02 to 40 x 12 in
- [ ] Mark bend line at 6 in from one long edge
- [ ] Brake at 30° (or 60° from vertical, same thing)
- [ ] Cut S-03 end caps to triangle matching fairing profile
- [ ] Rivet end caps inside fairing
- [ ] Drill bracket holes; attach B-01 angle brackets
- [ ] Bolt fairing assembly to F-02 leading rail

## Phase 6 — Finishing (Day 2, 30 min)
- [ ] Apply MAX CARGO 150 LB decal to top deck, visible side
- [ ] Insert T-slot cover strips in any unused channels (reduces whistle)
- [ ] Wipe entire platform with isopropyl

## Phase 7 — Mount platform to vehicle (Day 3, 30 min)
- [ ] Place platform on crossbars; center it
- [ ] Through bottom of platform extrusion, slide M8 channel nuts into the lower T-slot at each crossbar contact
- [ ] Install M8x25 cap screws with neoprene isolator washers up through Yakima bar mounting points into channel nuts
- [ ] Torque to **20 N·m**
- [ ] Wiggle test: platform should be rock solid

## Phase 8 — Road test (Day 3)
- [ ] Empty rack: 30 mph for 1 mile — listen for whistle
- [ ] 50 mph for 2 miles — listen for vibration, check torque after
- [ ] 70 mph for 5 miles — final check
- [ ] Re-torque all platform fasteners
- [ ] Test load with ShiftPod: drive same progression
- [ ] Test load with plywood: drive same progression at reduced 60 mph for first trip
