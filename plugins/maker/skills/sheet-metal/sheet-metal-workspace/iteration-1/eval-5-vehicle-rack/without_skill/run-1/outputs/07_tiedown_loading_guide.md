# Loading & Tie-Down Guide

## Universal rules
- **Never exceed 150 lb cargo** (decal on rack)
- **Always use minimum 4 straps** for any load
- **Strap rating ≥ 500 lb each**, cam buckle style preferred (ratchets can over-tension and crush soft cargo)
- **Drop-test** straps before driving — slap the cargo hard; nothing shifts
- **Re-check at first stop** (10 min in), then every fuel stop

---

## Loading the ShiftPod (Scenario 1)

```
                  FRONT OF VEHICLE
                  ────────────────
       ┌──────────────────────────────────┐
       │  Fairing                          │
       ├──────────────────────────────────┤
       │                                  │
       │   [   ShiftPod bag 50 x 14   ]   │  <- centered
       │                                  │
       │                                  │
       └──────────────────────────────────┘
              REAR
```

1. Place tent bag **centered** on platform, oriented front-to-rear (long axis fore-aft)
2. Pass strap 1 over tent at 8 in from front; anchor to T-slot on both sides
3. Pass strap 2 over tent at 24 in from front
4. Pass strap 3 over tent at 40 in from front
5. Add 1 lengthwise strap front-to-rear through tent's webbing handles, anchored to fairing bracket and rear F-02 rail
6. Cam-buckle each — pull until tent compresses slightly but bag is not crushed

## Loading plywood (Scenario 2)

```
                  FRONT OF VEHICLE
                  ────────────────
       ┌──────────────────────────────────┐
       │  Fairing                          │
       ├──────────────────────────────────┤
       │ ║                                 ║
       │ ║   4x8 plywood sheet             ║
       │ ║   24in overhang FRONT           ║
       │ ║                                 ║
       │ ║   16in overhang REAR            ║
       │ ║                                 ║
       └──────────────────────────────────┘
              REAR + RED FLAG
```

1. **Lay 2x 2x4 spreaders** crosswise on platform (one over each crossbar) — protects rack and gives strap purchase
2. Place sheet **good side up** on top of spreaders
3. Center side-to-side (2 in overhang per side)
4. Slide sheet so **front overhang ~24 in, rear overhang ~16 in** (keeps front-heavy for stability)
5. Straps:
   - Strap 1: over sheet, ~12 in back from front edge, both sides into T-slot
   - Strap 2: over sheet, mid-platform
   - Strap 3: over sheet, ~12 in forward from rear edge
   - Strap 4 (front anchor): cam strap from front edge of sheet down through windshield frame? NO — use a **front loop strap that goes through both front door jambs** OR anchor to factory tow eye
6. Attach **red flag to rear corner** of plywood
7. Drive ≤ 60 mph

## Loading 2 sheets of 1/2" ply (alternative)
- Same as above but 2 sheets stacked
- Use longer straps; tension separately so top sheet doesn't slide on bottom
- Add anti-slip pad (rubber shelf liner) between sheets

## Loading dimensional lumber (Scenario 5)
- Bundle 2x4s together with 2 straps **before** loading on rack
- Place bundle on rack with weight centered between crossbars
- 4 straps over bundle to platform T-slot
- 1 lengthwise strap through bundle to fairing and rear rail
- Red flag if rear overhang > 3 ft

---

## Strap anchor points on platform

The perimeter T-slot is open along the **outside face** of F-01 (long rails) at every point. Cam strap hooks go directly into the channel — slide along to position.

Recommended strap hook style: **flat T-hook** or **S-hook with safety latch**. Avoid skinny wire hooks (slip out of T-slot under load).

## Driving with load

| Speed | Notes |
|---|---|
| 0–30 mph | OK, no special precautions |
| 30–50 mph | Monitor side mirrors for cargo motion |
| 50–65 mph | Recommended max with tent |
| 65–70 mph | OK for tent only, snug straps |
| > 70 mph | Avoid; wind force increases as v² |

**Crosswind warning:** Flat platform + tall tent = sail effect. Reduce speed 10 mph in gusty conditions.

**Rain:** Tent bag is water-resistant; tighten straps after 20 min (webbing stretches when wet). Plywood — cover with tarp, strap tarp separately so flapping doesn't lift sheet.

---

## After arriving at makerspace

- [ ] Loosen straps but don't fully remove until ready to slide cargo
- [ ] Slide sheet goods to rear; one person on ladder receives, two below
- [ ] Inspect deck skin for new dents or strap-burn marks
- [ ] Re-tension any T-nuts that worked loose
- [ ] If returning empty, lock platform to crossbars (Yakima SKS) if leaving overnight
