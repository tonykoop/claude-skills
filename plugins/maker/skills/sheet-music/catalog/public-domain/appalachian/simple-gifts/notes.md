# Simple Gifts

Joseph Brackett wrote the tune and lyrics in 1848 as a Shaker dance
song. Most non-Shakers know it through Aaron Copland's 1944 ballet
*Appalachian Spring*, where it's a centerpiece. Both Brackett's
original and Copland's setting are public domain in the US (pre-1929
publication for the Shaker original; Copland's specific arrangement is
not what we use here — the canonical ABC traces back to the original).

## Provenance

- **Melody and lyrics:** Joseph Brackett, 1848 — public domain in the US.
- **This canonical ABC arrangement:** Tony Koop / Heifer Zephyr, 2026,
  derived from the 1848 Shaker hymnal melody, MIT-licensed.

## Form and notable features

AABA, 16 bars total in the canonical version. The tune sits in F major
in the canonical key, with a comfortable range (F4 to D5). Phrasing is
clear — every line ends on the tonic.

## Per-instrument transposition guide

| Instrument | Target key | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| NAF (A-tuned) | A minor pentatonic | Sounds wistful in minor; stick with the modal feel. |
| PVC flute | F major | Canonical key. Simple. |
| Drone flute | C major (drone tonic) | Transpose; drone implies C anchor. |
| Acoustic violin | F major or D major | F is canonical; D opens the strings. |
| Floor harp | F major | One lever change (B→Bb) for the harp's home C tuning. |
| Electric guitar | C, D, or G | Capo and re-key for open chord patterns. |

## Practice tip

The eighth-note runs in the B section trip up beginners — they want
to rush. Slow the entire tune down for the first 5-6 read-throughs.
Once you can run the B section at half tempo, accelerate gradually.
