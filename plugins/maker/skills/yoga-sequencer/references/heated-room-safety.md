# Heated-Room Safety

Use this reference for hot power, heated vinyasa, sculpt, C3-style, or any public class where heat and intensity shape the teaching plan. Keep the language practical and teacher-usable. Do not turn it into a waiver or medical disclaimer.

## Standard Checklist

Include a compact checklist near the class summary or modifications section:

- **Permission to downshift:** students may step instead of jump, skip vinyasas, take knees down, rest in child's pose, sit, or leave the room calmly if dizzy.
- **Hydration:** encourage students to arrive hydrated, sip when needed, and rejoin without rushing.
- **Breath-quality gate:** if breath becomes ragged, shallow, or forced, the advanced option is to reduce intensity.
- **No long breath retention:** keep breath continuous in the hot room.
- **Heat-distress signs:** name dizziness, tunnel vision, nausea, chills, goosebumps, confusion, unusual shortness of breath, glassy eyes, loss of coordination, or the feeling that the room suddenly got hotter.
- **Pregnancy / heat-avoidance path:** offer open twists, wider stances, supported balance, side-lying or bolstered rest, and non-heated alternatives.
- **Compression and inversion caution:** reduce or skip closed twists, deep belly compression, arm balances, and inversions when the room reads overheated, pregnancy-heavy, or unusually fatigued.

## Breath-Quality Gate

Use breath as the class's practical decision tool:

- If students can breathe steadily, they may keep the current layer.
- If breath gets ragged, they should reduce range, reduce pace, or skip the next vinyasa.
- If breath does not settle after downshifting, offer seated rest, supine rest, or a calm exit from the room.

Good teacher cue:

> If breath quality drops, that is your cue to simplify. In this room, downshifting early is advanced practice.

## Pregnancy And Non-Heated Substitutions

Do not diagnose or promise safety. Offer a different practice track:

- Replace closed twists with open twists or hands-to-hips variations.
- Replace deep compression with wider stances, supported lunges, or chair-pose holds.
- Replace arm balances and inversions with standing balance, boat-pose work, or supported core work.
- Replace belly-down shapes that feel restrictive with side-lying, seated, or supported alternatives.
- Offer side-lying rest, constructive rest, or bolstered savasana instead of flat supine rest when appropriate.
- If a student is avoiding heat entirely, suggest taking the non-heated version of the class rather than trying to force a hot-room plan to fit.

## Cue-Volume Arc In Heat

Teacher voice should usually get quieter as repetition and heat rise:

| Class phase | Cue volume | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Arrival | sparse | Set the heat contract, breath, and theme. |
| Warm-up | moderate | Students need joint landmarks and effort pacing. |
| First rhythmic flow | rhythmic | Breath-to-movement cueing keeps the room organized. |
| Repeated standing flow | rhythmic -> focused | Teach the first pass; use fewer words on the repeat. |
| Peak or focus work | focused | Short setup, clear exits, quiet attempt space. |
| Counterpose / cooldown | sparse | Let the nervous system come down. |
| Savasana | minimal | Rest does not need explanation. |

Use 2-3 anchor cues and repeat them instead of inventing new language when the room is hot. Heated rooms reward fewer words, steadier pacing, and clear exits.

## Worked Micro-Example

For a 60-minute hot power class with crow:

- Safety line: "If breath gets ragged or the room suddenly feels hotter, skip the next vinyasa and take child's pose or a seat."
- Peak path: crow prep, toe-tip crow, one-foot lifts, then crow only if the exit is calm.
- Non-wrist / pregnancy-aware alternate: boat-pose compression holds and supported chair, no arm balance.
- Cue arc: moderate in wrist/core prep, rhythmic in Sun A, rhythmic -> focused in standing repetition, focused and quiet during crow attempts, sparse after the peak.
