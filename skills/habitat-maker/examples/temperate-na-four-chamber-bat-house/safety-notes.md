# Safety Notes

## Shop Safety

- Wear eye, hearing, and respiratory protection while cutting plywood, MDO,
  cedar, grooves, and vents.
- Control dust; cedar and composite-panel dust can irritate lungs and skin.
- Clamp narrow spacer strips before cutting or drilling.
- Deburr passage holes and vent slots so bats do not contact splinters.
- Dry-fit before adhesive or sealant.

## Finish And Sealant Safety

- Use exterior finish only on exterior surfaces.
- Do not paint, stain, oil, or seal the interior.
- Keep adhesive and sealant squeeze-out out of roost chambers.
- Let exterior finishes cure fully before installation.

## Wildlife Safety

- Never disturb an occupied roost.
- Time maintenance for winter or the local non-occupancy window.
- Plan for guano below the open bottom.
- Avoid locations where pets, people, branches, or predators can contact the
  exit path.

## Mounting Safety

- Use hardware appropriate for the building or pole structure.
- Confirm the mount supports wet weight, wind load, and repeated thermal
  movement.
- Use a predator baffle on pole installations where climbing predators are a
  realistic risk.
