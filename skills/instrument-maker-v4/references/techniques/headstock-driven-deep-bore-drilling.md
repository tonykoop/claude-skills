# Headstock-Driven Deep-Bore Drilling

Use this technique for long straight bores in square or non-round woodwind
stock when the blank should not be spun between centers yet.

Source reference: Uncle Carpenter's pan-flute build video,
`https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6-cZA53_GV8`.

## What Changes

The usual lathe drilling setup is reversed:

- **Headstock:** Morse taper to Jacobs chuck to drill bit. The bit spins.
- **Tailstock:** custom vise or guided clamping fixture grips the square blank.
  The blank does not spin; the tailstock quill advances it into the bit.

This is useful for square or oversized blanks that are hard to hold
concentrically as rotating workpieces, especially before the outside has been
turned round.

## Best Fits

- Pan-flute tubes and siku/zampona pipes.
- Kena, quena, shakuhachi, fujara, and similar solid-blank woodwinds.
- Didgeridoo or long drone experiments when the bore is still drillable from
  one or both ends.
- Prototype chalumeau or reed-pipe bores when a straight cylindrical bore is
  needed before external turning.

For very long, tapered, or large-diameter bores, compare against split-blank
routing or staged boring from both ends before committing tonewood.

## Fixture Requirements

- Headstock Morse taper adapter that matches the lathe (`MT2`, `MT3`, etc.).
- Jacobs chuck with enough capacity for the pilot and finish bits.
- Rigid tailstock vise or square-stock carrier aligned to the spindle axis.
- Optional steady rest behind the tailstock carrier for long blanks.
- Positive anti-rotation feature in the vise: V-block, square pocket, or side
  clamps. Friction alone is not enough.
- Depth stop or marked drill shank so the bore length is controlled.

## Setup Checklist

1. Confirm the headstock taper and chuck capacity.
2. Mount the drill in the headstock chuck and check runout.
3. Clamp scrap stock in the tailstock vise, not the real tonewood blank.
4. Bring the blank up to the stationary bit and verify the center mark tracks
   the spindle axis.
5. Run a short pilot bore in scrap.
6. Measure entry location, exit location if through-bored, and bore wander.
7. Only then repeat on the prototype blank.

## Operating Procedure

1. Mark both ends of the blank with centerlines and bore center.
2. Start with a pilot bit. Keep speed conservative; long bits whip.
3. Advance with the tailstock quill in short pecks.
4. Retract frequently to clear chips. Heat and packed chips cause wander.
5. Step up through intermediate bits rather than jumping to final diameter.
6. Leave the bore slightly undersize if it will be reamed, lapped, or tuned.
7. Measure the bore, then turn the outside from the established bore axis.

## Safety Notes

- The tailstock workholding must be rigid. A loose square blank can grab and
  launch.
- Keep hands away from the spinning bit and chuck.
- Do not use high spindle speed with long drill bits.
- Back out immediately if the drill squeals, burns, or stops producing chips.
- Use eye protection and dust collection. Some tonewoods are sensitizers.
- Practice on pine or poplar before using expensive or oily wood.

## Packet Guidance

When this technique is selected for a build packet, include:

- Lathe taper and chuck spec.
- Tailstock vise drawing or fixture photo.
- Bore diameter sequence.
- Pilot/finish bit lengths.
- Peck depth and chip-clearing notes.
- Scrap validation row in `validation.csv`.
- Fallback plan if bore wander exceeds tolerance.
