## The cert gap (the real answer)

You're telling me that's not how it actually works for you on the floor — so I trust you over the profile. Two possibilities, and you'll know which:

Either way the practical move is the same — but it changes who you talk to.

## Can you route around the bandsaw?

Maybe — but not cleanly for this project. Here's what the bandsaw actually does on a paddle build, so you can decide:

| Op | Why bandsaw is the right tool | Workaround | Verdict |
|---|---|---|---|
| Rough-cut the blade profile from the blank | Curved cut through 1"+ ash, fast | Jigsaw with a long aggressive blade, or coping saw + lots of patience | Workable but slower and rougher |
| Resaw the blank to thickness if you start from a thicker board | Only the bandsaw resaws cleanly | Buy stock pre-dimensioned (4/4 or 5/4 ash from MacBeath) | Easy — just spend $ instead of time |
| Cut the bent loom curve after steam-bending | Clean curved cuts in springback-y stock | Hand saw + spokeshave cleanup | Painful but possible |
| Trim the grip end | Could use miter or hand saw | Miter saw, no problem | Easy |

Net: you *can* build a paddle without the bandsaw — it just turns the blade rough-out into a bigger hand-tool job (jigsaw + spokeshave + drawknife). For a one-off summer project that's not crazy. But if you're going to be in the woodshop a lot, getting the bandsaw clearance pays for itself instantly.

**My read:** sort the cert. Path A first (free, 15 min), Path B if needed.

## A few other things to plan around

These aren't blockers, just things to think about while you're sorting the cert:

- **Steam box.** Maker Nexus's woodshop doesn't (per the profile) have a stock steam-bending setup. You'll likely build a PVC-or-plywood steam box and use a wallpaper steamer or kettle. It's a small side-build — fine, lots of paddle-makers do exactly this.
- **Bending form.** You'll want a plywood form for the loom curve. The CNC is overkill but easy if you're cleared (`#cnc-cert`); otherwise a jigsaw + bandsaw + sander gets you there.
- **Ash sourcing.** Profile says members source from MacBeath in Berkeley — that's still your move for clear, straight-grained 8/4 white ash. Order in person if you can; paddle stock wants to be hand-picked for grain.
- **Time budget.** Realistic summer pace: 1 weekend on the form + steam box, 1 weekend roughing the blank, 1 day to bend (then leave it clamped a week), 2-3 weekends shaping with spokeshave + card scraper + sandpaper, 1 weekend finishing. Tight but doable.
