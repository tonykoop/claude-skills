# Safety & PPE

## PPE checklist

- [ ] Safety glasses (mandatory, all woodshop and CNC operations).
- [ ] Hearing protection (CNC + dust collection together is loud).
- [ ] N95 or P100 respirator. Baltic birch is mostly birch, but the glue
      lines in plywood release formaldehyde-bearing dust when cut.
      Ply dust is a respiratory irritant - mask up even with dust
      collection running.
- [ ] Closed-toe shoes.
- [ ] No loose sleeves, no jewelry, hair tied back.

## Machine-specific hazards

### Table saw (stock prep)
- Use a push stick for the rip. The 20 in width is fine, but anything
  under 6 in to the fence wants a push stick.
- Riving knife / splitter must be in place for through cuts.
- Confirm blade is square to table and fence is parallel before the
  first cut.

### CNC router
- **Never** reach into the work envelope while the spindle is energized,
  even if it's not moving. E-stop first.
- Verify work zero with an air-cut before committing the first toolpath.
- Don't use crescent wrenches on collet nuts - use the matched spanners.
- If the cut sounds wrong (chatter, screech, smoking), hit feed-hold,
  *then* spindle stop, *then* investigate.
- Watch for tool deflection on the V-carve - the bit is small at the
  tip and can wander if feed is too high.

### Sanding
- Random orbit sander dust port connected to vac.
- Sand outside or under the downdraft table if Maker Nexus has one.

### Finishing (Osmo Polyx-Oil)
- Oil-soaked rags can spontaneously combust. **Spread used rags flat
  to dry outdoors**, or submerge in water in a metal can with a lid
  before disposing. Do not ball them up in a trash bin.
- Use the finishing room or an area with airflow. Polyx-Oil is
  low-VOC compared to poly but still has solvent.

## Hazard summary table

| Hazard | Likelihood | Severity | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|---|
| Eye injury from chip ejection | Medium | High | Safety glasses, dust shoe |
| Plywood dust inhalation | High | Medium | N95+, dust collection on |
| Spindle / bit contact injury | Low | Very high | E-stop habit, no reaching in |
| Table saw kickback | Low | High | Push stick, riving knife, fence parallel |
| Hearing damage | High over time | Medium | Earmuffs/plugs |
| Oily-rag fire | Low | Very high | Spread rags flat outdoors to dry |
| Tab snap-off injury | Low | Low | Cut tabs with saw, not by flexing |

## Maker Nexus shop rules to confirm

This packet does **not** restate Maker Nexus's posted rules - their rules
take precedence over anything here. Things to verify before your session:

- [ ] CNC reservation system / sign-up.
- [ ] Whether the shop allows you to bring your own bits or requires
      use of the in-house tool crib.
- [ ] Material-storage policy (can you leave a half sheet between
      sessions?).
- [ ] Finish-room hours and ventilation rules.
- [ ] Required check-in/check-out procedure on the CNC.
