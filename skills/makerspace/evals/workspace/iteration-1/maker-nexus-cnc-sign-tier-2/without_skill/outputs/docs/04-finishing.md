# Finishing

## Finish chosen: Osmo Polyx-Oil 3032 (satin)

### Why this finish
- Penetrating hard-wax oil; doesn't film-build, so the V-carve detail
  stays crisp.
- Food-safe when cured; non-toxic to indoor air after cure.
- Easy to maintain - scuff and re-oil in years to come.
- Plays nicely with Baltic birch's open grain on the cut edges.

### Why not the alternatives
- **Wipe-on poly:** builds film, can pool in V-carve corners, looks
  plasticky.
- **Shellac:** beautiful but soft; not great for a sign that gets
  brushed by coats and bags by a front door.
- **Bare wood:** Baltic birch yellows unevenly with UV. Even one coat
  of oil dramatically slows that.
- **Paint:** out of scope; goal is to show the wood.

## Procedure

1. **Final inspection.** Sand to 220, blow out V-carves with compressed
   air, wipe with a clean dry microfiber. Do not use mineral spirits or
   water - either will raise the grain or interact with the oil.
2. **Coat 1.** Pour a small amount of Polyx-Oil onto a lint-free pad or
   white nylon abrasive pad. Work it into the surface in small circles,
   covering everything including the inside of the V-carves and all
   four edges. Less is more - Polyx-Oil cures by penetration; a thick
   coat will not cure correctly.
3. **Wipe back.** Within 20 minutes, wipe the entire surface with a
   clean dry microfiber until no oil reads on the rag. The wood should
   look "damp" not "wet."
4. **Cure.** Lay flat, face up, in a dust-free area for 8-12 hours.
   Maker Nexus finishing room or your home garage if it's clean.
5. **Light buff.** With 320 grit on a hand block (or a maroon Scotch-
   Brite), knock down any raised grain. Wipe clean.
6. **Coat 2.** Same as coat 1 but use about half the oil - the wood
   will absorb less the second time around.
7. **Wipe back hard.** Don't leave any standing oil.
8. **Cure 24 hours** before handling, 1 week before installation if
   you're picky.

## Rag disposal

Used Polyx-Oil rags are an autoignition hazard. Spread flat outdoors on
concrete or hang on a line until fully dry (rigid), THEN trash. Do not
ball them up in a bin.

this sign and a dozen more like it.

## Maintenance

- Wipe with a damp cloth as needed.
- Every 1-3 years (depending on wear), re-oil: 220 grit scuff, single
  thin coat, wipe back, cure overnight.
