# Photo shot list

For a portfolio-ready GitHub README, aim for 5-8 hero photos in a
consistent style. Shoot raw, edit later. Drop final files in this
directory and reference them from the top-level README.

## Shot list

1. **Hero / above-the-fold.** Sign mounted on a clean wall, three-
   quarter angle, soft side light. Letters readable, V-carve depth
   visible. This is the photo someone sees in the GitHub README.
2. **Flat lay - finished sign.** Top-down, on a neutral background
   (concrete, kraft paper, plain wood bench). Even light, no shadows
   in the V-carves.
3. **Detail - V-carve close-up.** Macro of the "W" or "M" showing
   the crisp edge of the V-carve and the natural birch face.
4. **Process - on the CNC mid-cut.** Time the shot for when the
   V-bit is partway through a letter. Dust shoe optional in shot;
   bit and chips in focus.
5. **Process - tabs visible just before release.** Shows the
   craft-of-the-cut, not just the finished part.
6. **Materials flat lay.** Bit, blank, tape, can of finish, calipers.
   Tools-of-the-trade shot.
7. **Back of the sign.** Keyhole slot + felt bumpers. Demonstrates
   you thought about installation.
8. **In situ.** Mounted by a real door or in a real entryway, with
   environmental context (a doormat, a coat hook, etc.).

## Lighting notes

- **Worst time to shoot finished wood:** noon overhead light.
- **Best:** soft window light at 45 degrees, or one diffused
  speedlight bounced off a wall.
- **The V-carve trick:** light needs to *rake* across the carve at
  a low angle to read the depth. Front-on light flattens it.

## Camera and post

- Phone is fine. iPhone 14+/Pixel 7+ files print at 8x10 cleanly.
- Shoot in raw if your phone supports it.
- Edit toward warm-neutral; Baltic birch yellows under most LEDs and
  can read green-cast if white balance is off.
- Crop to consistent aspect ratios (16:9 for wide hero, 1:1 for
  detail tiles, 4:5 for portrait).
