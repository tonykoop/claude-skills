# Drawing Brief — Climbing-Vine Plasma Trellis

Two SVG drawings ship with this packet. The brief below describes
each in full so a manufacturing-planner specialist can regenerate
or refine them from the design parameters.

## Drawing 1 — Overall silhouette and outline dimensions

**File:** `drawings/trellis-overall.svg`

**Sheet:** ANSI B (11 × 17), landscape, 1:8 scale (or 1:10 if
needed to fit dimensions cleanly).

**Title block (lower-right corner):**

- Project: Climbing-Vine Plasma Trellis
- Material: 1/4 in A36 mild steel
- Finish: Powder coat, RAL 9005 texture matte black
- Drawn by: Tony Koop / Maker Nexus
- Date: 2026-05-08
- Sheet 1 of 2
- Tolerance unless noted: +/- 0.060 in (1.5 mm) — plasma cut

**Views:**

- Primary: front silhouette, full part. Overall envelope rectangle
  shown as a phantom line: 24.000 wide × 86.000 tall.
- Critical dimensions called out:
  - Overall height (rail bottom to top tip) — 86.000 in
  - Overall width — 24.000 in
  - Bottom rail height — 4.000 in
  - Spike length below rail — 12.000 in
  - Trellis height above grade (rail top to top tip) — 70.000 in
  - Spike center positions — x = 6.000 in, x = 18.000 in
  - Smallest leaf-cusp interior radius — R 0.200 in TYP (TYPical)
  - Stem-rib root width — 1.500 in
  - Stem-rib tip width — 0.625 in
  - Tendril ribbon width — 0.500 in
  - Tendril minimum interior radius — R 0.300 in TYP

**Datum chain:**

- Datum A: bottom edge of the bottom rail (the spike-rail
  junction line).
- Datum B: vertical centerline of the trellis.
- All vertical dimensions chain from datum A.
- All horizontal dimensions chain from datum B.

**Notes block (lower-left corner):**

1. All curves are organic splines defined in the production CAM
   file. This drawing shows nominal dimensions only.
2. Plasma kerf compensation: 0.060 in nominal. Verify with kerf-
   test coupon (op-sequence step 4) before production cut.
3. Cut sequence: internal cutouts before perimeter. Final
   perimeter segment includes a bridge tab (sever after
   cooldown).
4. Edge finish: flap-disc bottom edge to remove dross. Wire-wheel
   both faces for mill scale. Sandblast prep at coater.
5. Mounting: spikes drive into prepared garden bed. Strike
   through a wood buffer only.

## Drawing 2 — Base / spike weld detail

**File:** `drawings/trellis-base-detail.svg`

**Sheet:** ANSI B, landscape, 1:2 scale on the detail.

**Views:**

- Detail of the bottom rail and both spikes, plus the rail-to-stem
  fillet.
- Section A-A: through one spike at its widest point, showing
  the 1/4-in plate thickness and the spike's tapered profile.
- Detail B: enlarged view of the rail-to-stem fillet showing
  the R 0.750 in minimum fillet radius.

**Critical dimensions:**

- Spike width at rail (root) — 1.500 in
- Spike taper angle (full angle) — 30 deg
- Spike length below rail — 12.000 in
- Rail-to-stem fillet radius — R 0.750 in MIN
- Plate thickness — 0.250 in (shown in Section A-A)

**Notes block:**

1. Spikes are integral to the cut silhouette — no weld step
   required if the production CAM file cuts them as one piece
   with the trellis.
2. If spikes are cut separately to save sheet, MIG weld per
   `op-sequence.md` step 10.

## How to regenerate

If the manufacturing-planner specialist needs to refresh these
drawings:

- Load `design.md` for the parametric inputs.
- Generate the silhouette spline in Fusion 360 / SolidWorks /
  OpenSCAD using the geometry rules in `design.md` "Geometry
  rules."
- Export the silhouette as DXF for the plasma CAM.
- Export the silhouette as SVG with dimensions for these
  drawings.
- The SVG files in `drawings/` are placeholders generated by
  hand from the design parameters; refresh them when CAD lands.
