# 06 - Finishing Plan

Powder coat will fail prematurely on a poorly prepared surface. Spend the time on prep; the finish lasts decades if you do.

## Step 1 - Deburr and edge cleanup (~45 min)

1. Knock off any micro-tab stubs flush with a 60-grit flap disc on a 4.5" angle grinder.
2. Run flap disc along **every cut edge** - both faces and the edge itself. Goal: remove dross, soften the burr, knock down the bevel just slightly. You're not polishing; you're making the edge round enough to hold powder.
3. Pay special attention to **inside corners of leaves** - dross collects there.
4. Inspect under bright light. Any sharp burr you can shave a fingernail on needs another pass.

## Step 2 - Mill scale removal (~30-60 min)

If you bought hot-rolled (likely), the surface has mill scale - bluish-gray oxide. Powder coat will adhere to it short-term but FAIL within a year as scale flakes off.

**Options:**
- **Sandblast** (best): in MN's blast booth if available, 80-grit aluminum oxide, full coverage both faces. ~30 min for a part this size.
- **Wire wheel + grind** (acceptable): knotted wire cup on angle grinder, both faces. Slower (~60 min), missed spots common, but works.
- **Chemical** (not recommended for this scale): phosphoric prep solutions exist but messy and the part is large.

After blasting, the surface is reactive - **flash rust starts in hours**. Move to coating same day.

## Step 3 - Degrease (~10 min)

1. Wipe entire part with acetone on lint-free rag.
2. Don't touch the part with bare hands after this - skin oils will telegraph through powder. Use clean nitrile gloves or hold by the stake portion (which gets buried).

## Step 4 - Powder coat (~2 hr including cure)

If using MN's powder booth:

1. **Pre-bake** at 400 F for 15 min to outgas the metal (plasma cuts trap surface contaminants; outgassing prevents pinholing).
2. Cool to ~150 F (still warm, not hot).
3. Hang from purpose-made hooks through tendril through-holes - these locations are intended hang points.
4. **Ground the hanger** to the booth - this is the entire principle of electrostatic powder.
5. Apply matte black powder. Two light passes are better than one heavy. Watch for Faraday-cage effect in tight inside corners (the leaf veins) - lower the gun voltage from default 50 kV to ~25 kV when reaching into recesses.
6. **Cure** per powder spec - typically 400 F for 20 min after part reaches temperature. Use a non-contact thermometer to confirm part temp, not just oven temp.
7. Cool slowly. Don't quench.

## Step 5 - Inspection

- No pinholes (would indicate outgassing failure - re-bake and re-shoot the affected area).
- No drips or thick zones.
- Coverage in inside corners (Faraday effect failure shows as bare metal in deep recesses).
- Edge coverage (powder tends to thin at sharp edges - which is why we softened them in Step 1).

## Touch-up

Bring leftover powder home. If install scratches happen, you can reheat a small spot with a heat gun and dust on touch-up powder, then reflow. Brush-on epoxy enamel matched to matte black is also acceptable for outdoor minor repair.
