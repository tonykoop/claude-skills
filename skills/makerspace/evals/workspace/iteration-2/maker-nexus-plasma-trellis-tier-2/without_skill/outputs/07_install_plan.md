# 07 - Install Plan

## Site prep

- Pick a location with at least 18" of soil clearance (no buried utilities, no large roots).
- Call 811 (or local equivalent) before digging if anywhere near property lines or utilities. Free service, mandatory in most jurisdictions.

## Anchoring approach (recommended)

The 6" stake portion of the trellis is NOT alone enough to keep a 72" lever stable in soil. Use rebar stakes flanking the spine.

1. Mark trellis location. The spine's bottom edge should be ~4" below grade once installed.
2. Dig a slot trench: ~6" deep, 8" long, 1" wide along the spine line.
3. Drive two 1/2" x 18" rebar stakes vertically, 12" apart, flanking where the trellis spine will sit. Drive 14" into solid ground, leaving 4" exposed.
4. Drop trellis into trench so the stake portion is buried; bracket plates straddle the rebar.
5. Bolt brackets to spine through the bracket holes (drilled, not plasma-cut, for snug fit) - 1/2"-13 SS carriage bolts, washers, nylock nuts. Brackets sandwich the rebar.
6. Backfill trench with original soil tamped in 2" lifts. For premium stability, mix in dry-pack concrete (no water - soil moisture cures it slowly) for the bottom 4".

## Alternative: surface-mount on a deck or planter

If installing on a deck or in a wood planter, omit rebar. Use 1/2" lag bolts through the bracket plates into the deck framing or a 4x4 brace inside the planter. Same brackets, same bolts.

## Plant training

- Use jute twine or soft plant tape - never wire (cuts stems).
- The two tendril through-holes are designed as primary tie-points. Add additional eye-screws elsewhere if the climber needs more support.
- Suitable climbers: clematis, sweet pea, morning glory, climbing nasturtium, light annual vines. AVOID: wisteria, climbing roses, mature grape - all develop crown weights that exceed this design's load envelope.

## Maintenance

- Inspect powder coat annually for chips, especially at ground line.
- Touch up chips immediately - exposed steel will rust under intact powder by capillary creep, lifting the coating outward.
- If installed in coastal salt-air zone, hose-rinse quarterly to clear chloride deposits.
- Expected service life on properly prepared and powder-coated A36: 15-25 years.
