# Assembly Manual — Heavy-Duty Waxed Canvas Tote

Step-by-step build guide. Photo placeholders are noted as
`[PHOTO: description]`. Capture these in-shop for the portfolio
upload.

## Phase 0 — Prep (before you arrive at the shop)

0.1 Pre-wash the cotton twill lining (M-02). Tumble dry low. Iron
flat after drying. **Do not pre-wash the waxed canvas** —
laundering dissolves the wax.

0.2 Print or sketch out pattern pieces from `drawing-brief.md`. If
you're tile-printing, tape the panels together before going to the
shop.

0.3 Confirm your `#textile-cert` status in the member portal. If
Maker Nexus has split the cert into a domestic/industrial
sub-cert structure since this packet was generated, schedule the
walking-foot check-out before your build session.

## Phase 1 — Cutting (~90 min, textile area cutting table)

1.1 Lay the waxed canvas wax-side-up on the cutting table.
Smooth out flat — no folds, no creases.

1.2 Position pattern pieces P-01, P-02, P-03, P-04, P-05 (×2) per
the cut-list nesting plan. Mark grain arrows. Use a clay marker
or chalk on the wax — washable markers don't take.

1.3 Cut P-01 through P-06 with a sharp 45 mm rotary cutter. Long
ruler for straight edges. Mark notches with 1/4" snips into the
seam allowance.

1.4 Mark strap-anchor crosshairs on P-01 and P-02 (canvas body
panels): 4" in from each side, 1.5" down from the top cut edge.
Use a tailor's chalk, mark on the inside (non-wax) face.

1.5 Lay out lining P-07 through P-12 on the cotton twill. Cut.

1.6 Cut foam P-13 ×2 with rotary cutter — 1.25" × 8".

1.7 (Optional) Cut HDPE stiffener P-14 in the woodshop:
   - Bandsaw to 5" × 17" rough.
   - Belt sand corners to 0.5" radius.
   - Wipe clean of HDPE shavings.

## Phase 2 — Lining + pocket (~60 min, domestic machine)

2.1 Hem the pocket mouth on P-10: fold the top long edge under
1/4", then under another 1/4". Topstitch 1/8" from the inner
fold. (Universal needle, Tex 40 thread, ~10 spi.)

2.2 Fold P-10 in half along its centerline, RST. Sew the two side
seams at 1/2" SA.

2.3 Turn the pocket right-side-out. Press flat. The bottom edge
is the fold; the top is the hemmed mouth.

2.4 Pin/clip the pocket to P-08 (back lining body panel),
right-side of pocket facing right-side of P-08, centered side-to-
side, top of pocket 4" down from top edge. Edge-stitch the two
sides and bottom of the pocket to P-08, leaving the top open.
**Reinforce the upper corners with a small box-X stitch** —
they're the highest-stress points.

`[PHOTO: pocket attached to back lining, before the lining is sewn together]`

2.5 Build the stiffener sleeve: place P-11 and P-12 RST, sew
three sides at 1/2" SA, leave one short end open. Turn right-side-
out. Press.

2.6 Position the sleeve on P-09 (gusset lining), centered along
the bottom 17" of the strip. Baste in place around three sides
(leaving the open end accessible from inside the bag).

2.7 Construct the lining shell: pin P-07 (front lining) to P-09
(gusset lining) RST, matching the side-edge midpoint notches to
the gusset corner notches. Sew at 1/2" SA, pivoting at the
corners. Repeat with P-08 (back lining) on the other side.
**Leave a 6" gap in one side seam, near the top, for bag-out
later.**

2.8 Press all lining seams open. Avoid pressing over the
stiffener sleeve.

`[PHOTO: lining assembled, inside-out, showing pocket and stiffener sleeve]`

## Phase 3 — Strap construction (~60 min, industrial walking-foot)

3.1 Take a P-05 strap blank (7" × 28"). Press it lengthwise: fold
both long raw edges in to meet at the centerline (creates a 3.5"-
wide piece with raw edges in the middle).

3.2 Fold again along the centerline (creates a 1.5"-wide strap
with all raw edges enclosed). Press.

3.3 Slide foam P-13 into the strap, centered along the length.
The foam goes between the two layers of folded canvas, occupying
the center 8".

3.4 Edge-stitch both long edges of the strap, 1/8" from the edge.
Use the walking-foot machine, 110/18 jeans needle, Tex 70 bonded
thread, 8-9 spi. Reduce speed where you cross the foam start/end —
the stack thickness changes.

3.5 (Optional) Add two parallel topstitch lines down each strap
for stiffness and looks: 1/4" inside the edge stitch. Tex 90
topstitch thread, 110/18 topstitch needle, 6-7 spi.

3.6 Repeat for the second strap. **Verify both straps are within
1/2" of the same length** before proceeding.

`[PHOTO: completed straps lying flat, showing foam-padded center]`

## Phase 4 — Strap anchor bartacks (~20 min, walking-foot)

4.1 Position one end of strap A on body panel P-01, centered on
the left strap-anchor crosshair. The strap end should overlap
1.5" onto the body panel.

4.2 Edge-stitch a rectangle around the strap end's footprint
(1.5" × 1.5" box). Then add an X across the rectangle's diagonals.
Then run a dense zigzag bartack across the top of the rectangle.
This is the box-X bartack.

4.3 Repeat for the other end of strap A on the right anchor of
P-01. **Make sure the strap isn't twisted.**

4.4 Repeat both anchors for strap B on P-02.

`[PHOTO: bartacked strap anchor, close-up showing the stitch density]`

## Phase 5 — Zipper install (~60 min, walking-foot, zipper foot)

5.1 Take the YKK #10 zipper. Trim the tape ends to leave 1/2"
beyond the metal stops at each end. Fold one P-06 zipper end tab
in half, wrap it over one end of the zipper tape, edge-stitch
across. Repeat for the other end.

5.2 Place P-01 (canvas body, front) right-side-up. Place the
zipper face-down on the top edge, with the tape's edge aligned
with the body panel's top cut edge. Place P-07 (lining body,
front) face-down on top, sandwiching the zipper.

5.3 Sew through all three layers along the top edge at 3/8" SA,
using the zipper foot. 110/18 jeans needle, Tex 70 thread.

5.4 Flip the canvas and lining away from the zipper. Press lightly
(press cloth on the canvas side). Topstitch 1/8" from the zipper
teeth on the canvas side, optional but recommended — keeps the
fabric from rolling into the zipper teeth.

5.5 Repeat 5.2-5.4 for the other side: zipper face-down on the top
edge of P-02 (canvas back), P-08 (lining back) face-down on top.

`[PHOTO: zipper sewn between canvas and lining, both panels open like a book]`

## Phase 6 — Shell construction (~60 min, walking-foot)

6.1 Sew P-04 (bottom reinforcement) to the *outside* of the
center 19" of P-03 (canvas gusset). Long basting stitch around
all four edges, then a permanent seam at 3/8" inside the cut edge
— top, bottom, and both ends.

6.2 With the zipper open, clip P-01 (canvas front, with zipper
attached) to P-03 (canvas gusset), RST, matching the
side-edge midpoint notches to the gusset corner notches.

6.3 Sew at 1/2" SA, starting at the top edge, down the side,
across the bottom, up the other side, ending at the top. **Pivot
at each gusset corner:** stop with the needle down, lift the foot,
clip the gusset's seam allowance into the corner, rotate, lower
foot, continue.

6.4 Repeat for P-02 (canvas back) on the other side of the gusset.

`[PHOTO: canvas shell turned inside-out, showing the boxed-corner construction]`

## Phase 7 — Binding the seams (~90 min, walking-foot)

7.1 With the canvas shell still inside-out, trim the seam
allowances to 3/8" and notch the gusset corners (a 1/4" notch
into the SA at each pivot point relieves bulk).

7.2 Wrap 1" double-fold bias tape around each seam allowance,
edge-stitch through all layers. The walking-foot will handle the
6-layer crossover at the corners; reduce speed there.

7.3 At the corner crossovers (where two bound seams meet), tuck
the binding tail under the perpendicular binding rather than
butting them — this hides the raw end and reinforces the joint.

`[PHOTO: bound seam interior, showing the bias tape coverage]`

## Phase 8 — Final assembly (~60 min)

8.1 Turn the canvas shell right-side-out. Push the corners out
with a chopstick or point turner. The bag now has its outside
shape.

8.2 Drop the lining (still inside-out) into the canvas shell. The
zipper tape is the only thing currently connecting them at the
top — you'll close the lining-to-zipper gap next.

8.3 Reach through the bag-out gap (the 6" gap you left in the
lining side seam during step 2.7). Pull the canvas shell through
it. Now the lining is right-side-out, sitting beside the canvas
shell.

8.4 Top-edge close: stitch the lining's top edge to the underside
of the zipper tape, just inside the existing zipper seam. This is
the final seam that joins the lining and shell at the top.

8.5 Hand-sew the bag-out gap closed with a ladder stitch, or
machine-stitch close to the seam — the gap is in the lining so
either is acceptable.

8.6 Push the lining down into the canvas shell. Top-edge should
roll cleanly with the zipper centered.

8.7 (Optional) Slide the HDPE stiffener (P-14) into the
stiffener sleeve through its open end.

8.8 Clip threads, light steam (lining side), inspect.

## Phase 9 — Re-wax (optional, ~15 min)

9.1 Identify high-wear seams: the bottom corners and the strap
anchors.

9.2 Rub the Otter Wax bar (M-14) over those areas.

9.3 Warm with a heat gun on low, keeping the gun moving so the
canvas doesn't scorch. The wax should melt into the fibers.

9.4 Buff with a clean rag.
