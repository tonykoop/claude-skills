# Operation Sequence — Heavy-Duty Waxed Canvas Tote

Ordered sequence of operations. "Machine" column maps to specific
Maker Nexus textile-area equipment.

| # | Op | Machine / tool | Foot | Needle | Thread | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Pre-wash lining (M-02) | At home or shop sink | — | — | — | Cotton twill shrinks ~3%. Tumble dry low. Do NOT wash waxed canvas. |
| 2 | Layout patterns on canvas, mark grain | Cutting table, chalk/soap | — | — | — | Use clay marker on wax side; chalk wipes off the wax. Mark all notch points and strap-anchor positions. |
| 3 | Cut canvas pieces P-01 through P-06 | Rotary cutter, self-healing mat | — | — | — | Sharp 45 mm blade. Long ruler. Cut on the wax side (mark side). |
| 4 | Cut lining pieces P-07 through P-12 | Rotary cutter, self-healing mat | — | — | — | Sharper blade easier here than scissors. |
| 5 | Cut foam P-13 ×2 | Rotary cutter or shears | — | — | — | EVA foam cuts cleanly with rotary; mind the blade. |
| 6 | (Optional) cut HDPE P-14 | Bandsaw (woodshop) or hand jigsaw | — | — | — | Woodshop bandsaw is the cleanest cut. Round corners with belt sander. Requires `#woodshop-cert` (you have it). |
| 7 | Press lining pieces flat | Iron + ironing board (textile area) | — | — | — | Steam OK on cotton. Set iron to cotton. |
| 8 | Hem pocket mouth (P-10) | Domestic sewing machine | Standard | 90/14 universal | Tex 40 | Fold top edge under 1/4"+1/4", topstitch. |
| 9 | Sew slip pocket onto back-lining body panel (P-08) | Domestic sewing machine | Standard | 90/14 universal | Tex 40 | Center 4" down from top edge. Box-X reinforce upper corners. |
| 10 | Build stiffener sleeve (P-11 + P-12) | Domestic sewing machine | Standard | 90/14 universal | Tex 40 | Sew 3 sides RST, turn, baste closed end to bottom of gusset lining (P-09) at center. |
| 11 | Construct lining: gusset to front, gusset to back | Domestic sewing machine | Standard | 90/14 universal | Tex 40 | Notch the gusset corners; sew with seam allowance gap at top (you'll bag-out through here). |
| 12 | Press all lining seams open | Iron | — | — | — | Don't press over the stiffener sleeve. |
| 13 | Build straps: fold, insert foam, edge-stitch | Industrial walking-foot | Walking foot | 110/18 jeans | Tex 70 bonded | This is where the walking foot earns its keep — 4 layers of canvas + foam in the middle. Edge-stitch both long sides 1/8" from edge. |
| 14 | Topstitch straps (optional aesthetic) | Industrial walking-foot | Walking foot | 110/18 topstitch | Tex 90 topstitch | Two parallel rows down each strap, 1/4" in from edge stitch. Improves stiffness. |
| 15 | Bartack strap anchors onto body panels (P-01, P-02) | Industrial walking-foot OR domestic w/ bartack capability | Walking foot or zigzag | 110/18 jeans | Tex 70 | Position per design.md — 4" in from each side seam, anchored 1.5" down from the top edge. Box-X bartack, dense zigzag, 30+ stitches. THIS IS A HIGH-STRESS JOINT — do not skip the bartack. |
| 16 | Sew zipper end tabs (P-06) onto zipper ends | Domestic or industrial | Standard or zipper | 90/14 | Tex 40 | Folded tabs cap each end of the 20" zipper tape. Trim tape to fit. |
| 17 | Sew zipper into top edge of body panels | Industrial walking-foot | Zipper foot | 110/18 jeans | Tex 70 | Zipper sandwiched between canvas body (right side) and lining body (right side). Sew the zipper to one body+lining sandwich first, then the other. Keep tension low — bonded thread + #10 zipper means thick stack. |
| 18 | Topstitch alongside zipper (optional) | Industrial walking-foot | Zipper foot | 110/18 topstitch | Tex 90 | 1/8" from the zipper teeth; helps the canvas roll cleanly when the zipper opens. |
| 19 | Attach bottom reinforcement panel (P-04) to outside of gusset (P-03) | Industrial walking-foot | Walking foot | 110/18 jeans | Tex 70 | Long basting stitch around all 4 edges, then full seam at 3/8". |
| 20 | Sew canvas body to canvas gusset, RST | Industrial walking-foot | Walking foot | 110/18 jeans | Tex 70 | Match notch points. Pivot at gusset corners (clip the seam allowance into the corner first). Both sides + bottom in one continuous seam. |
| 21 | Repeat for second body panel | Industrial walking-foot | Walking foot | 110/18 jeans | Tex 70 | Same procedure. |
| 22 | Bind seam allowances on canvas shell | Industrial walking-foot | Standard or binder attachment | 90/14 universal | Tex 40 | 1" double-fold bias tape (M-03) wrapped over the SA, edge-stitched both sides. The walking foot handles the binding stack better than domestic. |
| 23 | Turn canvas shell right-side-out | Hand work | — | — | — | Push out corners with a chopstick or point turner. |
| 24 | Drop lining inside canvas shell, wrong sides together | Hand work | — | — | — | Match top edges; clip with wonder clips. |
| 25 | Connect lining top edge to zipper tape underside | Industrial walking-foot | Zipper foot | 110/18 jeans | Tex 70 | This closes up the bag. Stitch through canvas + zipper tape + lining all at once, just inside the existing zipper seam. |
| 26 | (Optional) drop in HDPE stiffener (P-14) | Hand work | — | — | — | Insert through top opening, into stiffener sleeve. |
| 27 | Hand-finish: tuck thread tails, snip, light steam | Hand work + iron | — | — | — | Mind the wax: press from lining side or use a press cloth. |
| 28 | (Optional) spot-rewax high-wear seams | Otter Wax bar + heat gun | — | — | — | Outdoor or well-ventilated. Gentle warm air, no flame. |
