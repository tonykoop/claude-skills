# Safety Notes — Heavy-Duty Waxed Canvas Tote

Sewing looks tame next to woodworking but every machine in the
textile area has its own way of biting — and the industrial
walking-foot is markedly more capable of injuring you than a
domestic machine.

## PPE

- **Safety glasses** — yes, even for sewing. Industrial machines
  run at high SPM (stitches per minute); broken needles are
  projectiles. Maker Nexus rule: glasses any time a tool is
  running (`safety-rules.md`). Sewing machines count.
- **Closed-toe shoes** — shop floor rule applies in the textile
  area too.
- **Hair tied back** — feed mechanisms can grab loose hair near
  the throat plate.
- **No loose sleeves or jewelry** near the needle bar.
- **Light dust mask** if you're spending an extended session
  with waxed canvas — wax-impregnated dust during cutting is
  mild but worth respecting.

## Industrial walking-foot — specific risks

The walking-foot is faster, stronger, and less forgiving than a
domestic machine.

| Risk | Why it happens | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| Needle through finger | Compound feed pulls fabric *and your hand* if you're holding too close. | Keep fingers at least 2" from the needle. Use wonder clips or basting, not pinning. Slow speed for tricky areas — the knee/foot pedal modulates speed; learn it before running production seams. |
| Eye strike from broken needle | 110/18 needles can break crossing thick stacks (strap anchors, gusset corners). Pieces can fly. | Safety glasses. Reduce speed at thick stacks. If you hear the needle protest, stop and reposition. Let the walking foot do the work — don't shove fabric. |
| Pinch / crush in the walking-foot mechanism | The foot lifts and lowers in sync with the feed; fingers under it during operation get pinched. | Hands stay on top of fabric, never under the foot. Lift the foot lever (knee or hand) to reposition; don't try to slide fabric while running. |
| Belt-driven motor pinch (clutch motors) | Industrial machines often have an exposed v-belt between motor and head. | Don't reach behind/below the head while running. Power down with the master switch before clearing jams. |
| Repetitive strain | Long sessions on a machine that's faster than you're used to. | 5-min break every 45 min. Adjust seat height so elbows are at 90°. |

## Wax and heat

- **Don't iron the wax side directly** at high heat. The wax
  melts at ~150-160°F and will stain the iron and fabric. Use a
  press cloth or press from the lining side at "wool"
  temperature.
- **No open flame on waxed canvas.** Includes lighters, gas
  ranges, candles. Wax + cotton = flammable.
- **Re-waxing with a heat gun** (op #28) — outdoor or
  well-ventilated. Heat gun on low setting, keep moving. Don't
  let the canvas scorch (smell of burnt cotton = back off
  immediately).

## Fume / exposure

- Waxed canvas is mostly cotton with a beeswax/paraffin blend —
  low toxicity. No respirator needed for normal cutting/sewing.
- Bonded polyester thread does not present a fume hazard at
  normal sewing speeds.
- HDPE stiffener: no machining concerns at this scale. The
  bandsaw cut produces fine HDPE chips — clean up with shop
  vac, not compressed air.

## Sharp tools

- **Rotary cutter** — kept locked when not in active cut.
  Treated like a pizza wheel, but it's a razor. Cut away from
  yourself, on a self-healing mat. Don't rest the open blade
  on the table.
- **Shears / scissors** — fabric shears stay in the textile
  area; never use them on paper (dulls them).
- **Seam ripper** — point away from your other hand. They're
  small but they *will* draw blood.

## Maker Nexus shop policy alignment

- Buddy system: not strictly required for textile work, but
  after-hours solo sessions on the industrial machine are
  worth a check-in via the textile-area channel — same
  principle as woodshop after-hours.
- Incident reporting: needle through a finger is a
  reportable incident (`safety-rules.md` — "any injury,
  near-miss, or equipment damage"). Not punitive; the
  shop logs it so others learn.
- E-stop: the master switch on the industrial machine head
  is your e-stop. Find it before you start running seams.

## After the build

- Sweep cutting table — wax-impregnated cotton dust gets
  tracked around.
- Return rotary cutter, shears, clips to their stations.
- Wipe down machine bed.
- Empty bobbin if you swapped to project-specific thread.
- Log the build in your member portal if Maker Nexus tracks it.
